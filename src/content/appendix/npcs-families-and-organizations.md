## Fiona Scarlett (Cleric 1 -- Sharess)

![A person in a dress Description automatically

generated](/Daggerdale/images/chapter7/image82.jpeg)

**Special Ability: Nearness of Sharess**

Fiona is imbued with Sharess's divinity in some small measure. People
coming very near to her, or in contact with her will feel slightly
euphoric. She can give *Bless* one character/day with a gentle action
(holding a hand, kiss on the cheek), which will give that character **+1
to attack rolls and will saves for the remainder of the day.**

**Working with Mari One Day...**

Fiona Scarlett is very eager to please. She helps you tidy things up at
Embassy, and you can see that she is accustomed to common work; she is
adept at cleaning, cooking, mending, and especially to seeing to the
needs of patrons; she happily brushes and braids your hair, and you feel
very confident and contented afterwards.

During the course of these activities, she opens up to you, and you
learn the following about her;

She is short and curvy, at 5'1", and not particularly athletic. She has
naturally blonde hair, but it is dyed black today, and she favours
wearing lots of makeup to match the colour of her hair. She goes on and
on about the dressing table she had at The Old Fort in Charity, and how
she could apply any colour to her face or hair, and you can see that she
misses it.

She is an orphan and never knew her parents. The surname 'Scarlett' in
Charity denotes her status as an orphan, just like the surname 'Dale' in
the rest of the Dalelands. In Charity, it is sign that you are likely
not free, and is a stigma, whereas many boast of their surname of Dale,
as being emblematic of their self-reliance.

Fiona was found on the steps of Charnel Priory 16 years ago and was
raised by the Sisters until the age of 8. She showed no promise as a
Sister and was assigned to live with the Keystone family. Her adopted
father, Walter Keystone, was the Merchants' Guildmaster, as well as
Speaker of the Common Assembly. He was one of the most influential
people of his caste ('Middlemen'), but was revealed recently to be part
of a cult known as The Gideon Cult, and is at-large as a wanted man. The
family descended quickly into penury; Fiona was turned out, her 'papers'
(you think this is something of an ownership contract or some such)
signed over to Medea Rowan at The Old Fort.

She had been a novitiate in the church of Sharess since the age of 12,
and was already well known to Medea, and was a promising girl with
enthusiasm and a natural affinity for people and her goddess that made
her stand apart from other girls her age. However, she never got
accustomed to the rougher men of the Old Fort, and she knew that her
time would soon be coming to minister to her patrons in a more intimate
manner. Some of the older girls told her that there were many men asking
when she would be available, and that she should be careful. It was well
known that the dilletante Godwin Ferguson asked after her all the time,
and that Mme. Medea had refused great inducements to turn her out early
from Godwin.

Hearing that there was a large group of merchants and dignitaries
leaving for Dagger Falls, she decided to stow along, and left The Old
Fort and Charity without leave. This is a punishable offense, given that
she is not 'Free' to leave by their laws. She was horrified to hear that
among the nobles in the caravan headed for the Falls was Godwin himself.
She did her best to blend in with the rest of the 100 or so people in
the caravan, but was ultimately found out, and brought before Godwin,
while they were camped at Crossed Blades farm.

She had a frightful discussion with him, that she is hesitant to give
any details about, but she insists that he is a bad man, who has a great
deal of leverage over her; she has broken the law, and only by his
graces will she ever be truly free of Charity and her indentured
servitude.

The caravan arrived in the Falls a few days ago, and Fiona attempted to
escape her bonds again. She has been sleeping in alleyways for a couple
of nights but was found this morning (the 19<sup>th</sup>) by some of the men
from Charity, and brought before Ferguson. Now, having crossed him and
his laws on multiple occasions, and being brought before a clearly-drunk
and rapacious young noble, she feared the worst sort of treatment. But
whether Ferguson was already slaked, or driven by some momentary bout of
compassion, he told her that if she could get a meeting with The
Chrysalis that he would free her.

The rest you know.

## Standing Stone League

This name is a reference to the Standing Stone erected 1483 years ago.
History;

> *Located deep within the forest of Cormanthor, the Standing Stone was
> originally, after its construction, not accessible by humans. However,
> when Raythauvyr of [Sembia](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Sembia) demanded
> a road be built through the region, under threat of war, it was routed
> to pass within viewing distance of the monument. This gesture was
> meant to exemplify the lack of need for violence required from the
> allies of elves to achieve what they so
> desired.[<sup>\[7\]</sup>](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Standing_Stone#cite_note-FRCS3e-124-7)*
>
> *In the 900s [DR](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/DR), the
> region around the Standing Stone succumbed to more frequent and
> vicious attacks by the
> bandit-lord [Galath](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Galath),
> led from a nearby overlook. He and his forces were thwarted by a group
> of [mages](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Mage) assembled
> from merchants of the nearby, rapidly growing nation of
> Sembia.[<sup>\[8\]</sup>](https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Standing_Stone#cite_note-VGtD-190-8)*
>
> *On [Flamerule
