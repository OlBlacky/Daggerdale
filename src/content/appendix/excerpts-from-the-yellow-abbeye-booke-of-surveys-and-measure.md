**Population:** 440

**Non-Humans:** 39 (12 dwarves from one family and some workers, 24
halflings from 2 families, 3 half orcs of reasonable disposition)

**Governance**

Mayor Latimer Winters, 4th Co. Sun Blades Foresters (retired), aged 49,
council of 5 elders elected biannually, must include one Chauntean.

          Magistrate Sammual Saddler (c.f. Yellow Abbeye Grande
Accounting of the Clergy, 1480 Edition), aged 41, 4 clerks in Rotation.

**Military**

          7th Sun Blade Bright Spears, 1^st^ & 2^nd^ Platoons, under
command of Lt. Maddy Riverstone, aged 36.

          Militia above-strength at 60 men and women in good order, more
than half former Sun Blades

**Real Holdings**

          Class V fortification, barracks for 200 (finest fortification
outside of Town or The Castle)

          Fortified holdings; 11 acres inside class V perimeter,
fortified redoubt only (no wall around nearby structures), Class III
fortifications at Bridgegate and East Watch each containing roughly 5
acres of buildings, barns and gardens.

          Cultivated holdings; 715 acres, 1/3rd each in rotation of
Wheat, Barley, fallow.  65 acres of private gardens with all known
varieties of vegetable, fruits and herbs. 

**Locations of Interest**

**Bridgegate:** This is a small hamlet at the western end of the
settlement, about a mile away from the main village. There are 41 people
here that live in a small fortified farm that controls access to the
bridge which crosses the Sukiskyn River, which comes down the escarpment
and joins the Tesh here.

**East Watch:** This is a small hamlet at the eastern end of the
settlement a little over a mile away from the main village. There are 29
people here plus a platoon of Sun Blades. The village sits on a small
knoll in an elbow of the Tesh, and overlooks both the river and the road
which passes by, some 300 yards to the north. There is a stone tower
here and a barracks for an entire company of Sun Blades, and the 1^st^
platoon is usually stationed here; the first real defenders on the
eastern edge of Daggerdale.

**Banshee's Lament:** This is a deep and complicated cave system that
has been used as a copper mine from time to time over the centuries. The
depths or extent of the caves are unknown to the men of the Dale, and it
is supposed that it connects with the vast network of limestone caves in
the region, and likely the Underdark.

**Gribbons Perch (Castle & Cathedral):** This is an impressive
dwarf-laid fortress that was built between 1441-1465. It has been built
completely over a small island, and stands 20' or more from the surface
of the river in most places. Fortified bridges on the east and west side
secure entry and egress, and there is always a significant body of the
2^nd^ platoon on duty here, as well as a number of militia. The only
day-to-day activities that take place at the castle for most residents
are services for Amaunator, which many residents attend.

**The Village:** Surrounding the Perch on the north side of the river is
the village of Gribbons' Farm. There are about 200 people living here in
well-made homes, most 2 stories high, with a small number of successful
businesses and skilled artisans.

**Autumnhill Terrace Inn **-- Built on a small stone terrace on the edge
of the river/moat opposite the castle, this inn is a good size and of
good quality. Barns and paddocks at the edge of the village can
accommodate any number of animals, and this is a favourite stopping
point for caravans who want to do as much business as they can before
being taxed at the gates of Dagger Falls.

**Rooms and Food**

- Common Room cot (20 total) -- 1 sp/night

- Private Room -- sleeps 4 (10 rooms) -- 6 sp/night

- Private Room -- sleeps 2 (4 rooms) -- 4 sp/night

- Local wine (Autumnhill Terrace House Red) -- 3 cp/glass, 3 sp/bottle

- Pint of ale -- 1 cp

- Breakfast (porridge, eggs, sausages, mushrooms, tomatoes, toast and
  jam) -- 2 cp

- Lunch (rye bread, butter, potatoes, sausages, beans) -- 2 cp

- Dinner (mutton stew or fresh fish, vegetables in garlic butter, sour
  dough rolls, tea and/or ale) -- 3 cp

**Proprietors --** Wendor & Breezy Autumnhill (halfling family)

**Fish Market: F**rom Spring until the first snows, a small market
operates in the village that sells fresh fish for excellent prices.
Local fishermen catch quite a bit bass, sunfish and eels, which can be
bought fresh or packed here.

**Hamlets:** This farm has 4 other small hamlets besides the Bridgegate
and East Watch. Being in close proximity to Dagger Falls has allowed for
the braver settlers to establish small hamlets of a few families one-two
miles away from the main village. A few abandoned or ruined ones also
dot the landscape here.
