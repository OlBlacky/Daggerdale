A quiet moment on the march to Thistle Ridge. Lucan is only vaguely
paying attention to his compatriots as they file towards their destinies
on this fateful Greengrass night. Around the party, the light of the
almost-full moon bathes the woodland in an ethereal silver glow, casting
long shadows that carry a sense of foreboding. The air carries the sweet
perfume of countless blossoms---hawthorn and wild cherry erupting in
clouds of white and pale pink against the darkness. Tiny wildflowers
carpet the forest floor in patches of color that seem to shimmer under
Selûne's watchful eye---blue periwinkles, golden celandine, and the
delicate white stars of wood anemones.

The sprites accompanying Chrysalis are in their element, flitting
between the blooms and trailing faint luminescence in their wake,
clearly reveling in this night of natural power. In every direction, the
evidence of winter's retreat is unmistakable---tender green shoots push
through the soil, leaf buds unfurl on branches that were bare only a
week earlier, and the occasional nightingale's song pierces the quiet
with a complex melody. Patches of frost cling stubbornly to shadowed
hollows, but even these last bastions of winter seem to be surrendering
to the inevitable tide of renewal.

Signs of spring are everywhere to be seen. The end of death, the
awakening of life. An eternal cycle. Lucan remembers a tale he heard
from one of the old salts he spent his youth with on the docks of
Saerloon. "That which is not dead can eternal lie\..." He thinks of the
ghosts and the sacrifices that Palus engineered in Thistle Ridge. How
did the couplet go? "And in strange aeons, even death may die." Death is
not the end, clearly---but then, maybe it isn't the beginning either. He
stops suddenly, which brings Jesra flitting over to check on him.

**Lucan:** *\[his eyes suddenly gleaming\]* Jesra! That
book---Baervindour's tome on the fey. The section on hagspawn! I've just
had a revelation about Dorian---Lobelia's supposedly departed twin.

**Jesra:** *\[tilts her head sideways\]* Departed? Mortals use such
final words for things that merely change shape or step behind a
curtain. What bright spark has caught your eye now, Stone Tree?

**Lucan**: *\[not really paying attention\] *That text that Invictus
sent expressly stated that the *male* offspring are the true
Changelings---the actual shape-shifters! And while the mothers typically
kill the males---not unsensible from their perspective, I
suppose---sometimes they escape in a manner "made possible usually by
rival Fey." Those were the exact words.

**Jesra**: *\[smiles faintly\]* A rival's thorn can become another's
flower. Or a hidden dagger. Fey rescue fey for reasons like tangled
roots -- hard for mortals to trace.

**Lucan**: Precisely! And who is Palus's greatest rival? Her sister,
Quercina! The dryad Contessa whose power Palus sought to usurp! *\[Lucan
continues voicing his thoughts as he begins walking more quickly to
catch up with the rest of the party.\] *So, here's the thing: what if
Lobelia and Dorian were true twins? I mean - what if they
were *both *hagspawn? Dorian supposedly died in that 1439
sacrifice---the one orchestrated by Palus as 'The Ancient Mother.' But
it's only the story about the hunting accident that mentions him by
name. And in any case, we only have Abrecia's ghost-word that the
sacrifice claimed all three victims.

**Jesra:** Ghosts remember pain clearly. Sometimes they forget the
escape hatch. Or perhaps the cage *was* locked for all.

**Lucan:** *\[dismissively\]* If there's one thing I know
from Saerloon's docks, it's that witnesses see what they expect to see.
Believe me. As it happens, I've made use of that fact to some profit.
Now, what if Quercina caught wind of her sister's plans? What if she
rescued Dorian, snatching away the male Changeling from his mother's
attempt to take him off the board before he came into his powers? A true
Changeling could easily shift his form and fake his death. *\[his eyes
gleam with the thrill of realization.\] *He could still be out there!

**Jesra:** *\[Tilts her head\]* A hidden pawn on the board? Or merely a
lost seed blown away by the wind? Perhaps. But Stone Tree, the other
shifters you chase, the ones whispering in shadows\... do they offer you
friendship? Or sharp teeth? A rescued Changeling might still bear the
mother's chill, or wear the rescuer's gloom. A shape-shifter might
forget his own face after a time, especially one born of
such\... *mixed* soil.

**Lucan:** *\[waves impatiently\]* Spare me your fey bigotry. I'm
speaking of motivation and strategy, my little night-light, not
miscegenation. The point is, he *could* have survived! If she discovered
her sister was scheming against her, Quercina had motive. She would have
had every reason to spite her---and maybe secure an asset, to boot.
Dorian had the means to disappear completely---inherent Changeling
abilities, like the book said.

**Jesra:** (Shrugs delicately, a movement like rustling leaves) Perhaps.
Or perhaps the mother *did* tidy her garden, just as the old words say.
A snapped twig is still a snapped twig, even if another tries to graft
it elsewhere. Fate finds its way. You say Quercina now is "dark and
dangerous," brooding in her mine. A Changeling raised
in *that* shadow\... would he bring sunlight, Stone Tree? Or just a
different kind of storm?

**Lucan:** *\[rolling his eyes\]* Not that I don't appreciate your
fey... perspective, but we investigators prefer facts and logic. A
Changeling twin, rescued by a rival Fey, potentially still alive, hidden
by his own powers! Quercina is brooding because Palus wronged her!
Dorian would naturally be opposed to Palus, too! An enemy of our
enemy\... *\[with growing excitement\]* Once we've wrapped up things in
Thistle Ridge, we'll need to speak with Quercina herself. Find that cave
she's holed up in. Maybe she'll remember.

**Jesra:** *\[Shakes her head slowly, a touch of pity in her
eyes\]* Mortals love straight lines in tangled woods. Be careful the
path you think you see isn't just a circle leading back to the start,
Stone Tree. Can you be sure the ally you seek isn't just another mask
worn by the same old shadows? Some rescues only change the cage. Now,
are there any sweet berries left? All this thinking makes one hungry.

**Lucan:** *\[with a confident smirk\]* When have I ever led us astray?

He reaches into his pack and tosses a handful of berries to Jesra. They
begin spinning around her head like a halo. She plucks one from the
orbit and pops it in her mouth, then flits off to dance in the moonlight
with her sisters.

## Dossier Entry: Palus & The Ancient Mother - A Singular Malice

**Subject:** *The interconnected web of deceit surrounding the
sacrifices of 1439 and 1441 DR, the hidden temple, and the true identity
of the architect behind Thistle Ridge\'s woes, including the fraudulent
use of noble Sembian family names.*

**Analysis:** *The sequence of tragedies afflicting Thistle Ridge,
particularly the sacrifices involving individuals masquerading under
respected Sembian names like Alavandor and Clarandal, presents a puzzle
that lesser minds might find intractable. It has become increasingly
clear through my investigations that these settlers were likely
impostors, adopting established family names for reasons yet unknown --
perhaps simple deceit to avoid scrutiny or lend themselves an air of
legitimacy they did not possess. Regardless of their true origins, their
fate was intertwined with a singular malice. Through rigorous analysis
of seemingly unrelated testimonies and discoveries, a clear -- and
frankly, chilling -- picture emerges. It falls to me, naturally, to
illuminate this truth.*

**Key Evidence & Deductions:**

**The Hidden Temple:** Our discovery of the concealed shrine, confirmed
by Brother Vesuvian\'s (admittedly adequate) research to possess Sembian
architectural hallmarks, places its likely construction concurrent with
the arrival of these *so-called* Alavandor and Clarandal families, circa
1408-1409 DR. This timing is crucial.

**The Palus Likeness:** Our fey patron, Contessa Calix, reported --
albeit with typical fey vagueness -- that one of the statues within this
very temple bore a resemblance to Contessa Palus *as she appeared in the
past*. If the temple dates to \~1409, this places Palus\'s influence, or
at least her symbolic presence, at the very inception of these
impostors\' interaction with this site.

**The Temple\'s Dedication:** The iconography points towards the
Netherese entity Jannath, often represented in a triple aspect
(Crone/Mother/Child). This aligns with the entity blamed for
the *first* sacrificial deception targeting these settlers.

**The 1439 Sacrifice:** The spirit of Abrecia (who used the Alavandor
name) explicitly blamed \"The Ancient Mother\" for the trickery that led
to her demise and that of Andarl and Dorian (also operating under false
names). This directly implicates the entity associated with the hidden
temple in the events of 1439.

**The 1441 Sacrifice:** Contessa Palus, the Swamp Hag, reportedly
confessed (again, via Calix) to orchestrating the *second* sacrificial
deception in 1441, which claimed the lives of the three young girls
associated with these families.

**Synthesis & Conclusion:**

The less astute might view these as separate tragedies orchestrated by
different entities -- \"The Ancient Mother\" (Jannath?) in 1439 and
Palus in 1441. This is demonstrably illogical when considering the
evidence in totality.

Why would a temple built by these Sembian impostors around 1409 contain
a statue resembling Palus, unless she was involved from that early
stage, perhaps even complicit in their deception?

Why would the entity worshipped or represented *at that temple* (\"The
Ancient Mother\") be blamed for the first sacrifice, while Palus
confesses to the second, nearly identical deception?

The inescapable conclusion, reached through my own deductive reasoning,
is that Contessa Palus and \"The Ancient Mother\" are functionally the
same entity in this narrative, or, at the very least, Palus has been
operating under the guise or influence of this entity since these
impostors arrived.

She likely manipulated these settlers from the outset, perhaps
encouraging the construction of the temple (or allowing her likeness
within it) while masquerading as, or channeling, Jannath/\"The Ancient
Mother.\" This allowed her to orchestrate the 1439 sacrifice under that
guise, later revealing her hand more directly (or simply being
identified differently by Calix) when orchestrating the 1441 Tragedy.

This reveals not merely a hag\'s opportunism, but a calculated,
decades-long campaign of manipulation targeting these settlers and the
very foundations of Thistle Ridge. The hag Palus is not
merely *a* villain in this saga; she is the prime architect of its core
tragedies.

**Implications:** Understanding this singular, long-term malice is
paramount. It confirms Palus as the central antagonist whose influence
predates the commonly known Tragedy. Any strategy to cleanse Thistle
Ridge or bring peace to the spirits must target Palus directly,
acknowledging the depth and duration of her machinations against all who
settled there, legitimate or otherwise.

## Dossier Entry: The Grand Conspiracy---Netheril, Palus, and the Pawns of Thistle Ridge

**Subject:** *A comprehensive synthesis detailing the decades-long
conspiracy orchestrated by Netheril and the Fey-turned-Hag, Contessa
Palus, utilizing cursed settlers under fraudulent identities to
destabilize Thistle Ridge, potentially followed by a betrayal that
shapes the current conflict.*

**Analysis:** *It falls, inevitably, to an intellect such as mine to
navigate the labyrinthine mess of clues left scattered across time and
testimony. While my companions often stumble upon clues like truffle
pigs rooting in the mud, mistaking correlation for causation, or getting
lost in sentimental tangents, it requires a mind like mine -- sharp,
analytical, and unburdened by excessive sentiment -- to assemble these
fragments into the undeniable truth. Lesser minds might gloss over
details, but true understanding lies in the specifics. Conversely, while
others fixate on isolated clues, only I have perceived the grand design.
The events at Thistle Ridge were no mere sequence of unfortunate
incidents; they were the calculated moves in a game played by masters of
shadow and deceit, with the so-called Alavandor and Clarandal settlers
serving as little more than disposable pawns on the board. The sheer
elegance of the conspiracy, once unveiled, is almost admirable, were it
not so steeped in treachery.*

**Phase 1: The Netheril-Palus Alliance & Manipulation (\~1408 - 1441
DR)**

1\. **Netheril\'s Opening Gambit:** The foundation of this entire affair
rests on Netheril\'s ambition. Seeking to undermine Daggerdale, their
agents operating within occupied Sembia (specifically Daerlun, a crucial
link I discerned, given its status as a Netherese puppet-state)
identified or engineered a group afflicted by a sterility curse --
perfect leverage. They then orchestrated the removal of
the *true* families bearing the Alavandor and Clarandal names (the
\'AA\' letter confirms this forced vanishing, speaking of an
\"agreement\" to \"vanish forever\") and installed these cursed
impostors in Thistle Ridge under the stolen identities, luring them with
the promise of a cure. A masterful (if clichéd) setup, placing indebted
agents in a key location. Father would admire it, certainly.

2\. **Palus\'s Calculated Entry:** Contessa Palus, originally
Quercina\'s Fey sister, was Netheril\'s local instrument. The evidence
compels the deduction that her alliance with the Shadow *predates* the
settlers\' arrival (c. 1408). How else to explain her immediate
influence and the statue bearing her likeness in the shrine they
constructed, apparently depicting her appearance *before* her later
transformation? This pre-existing pact, likely sealed with the sacrifice
of her own kin (a chilling detail confirming her ruthlessness, though
the exact timing remains secondary to the fact of the betrayal),
positioned her perfectly to manipulate the newcomers. Her eventual
transformation into a hag, I suspect, was a price paid or perhaps a
consequence embraced, likely accelerating after the 1439 events and
culminating around 1441.

3\. **The Guided \"Cure\":** The settlers, desperate to continue their
lines, fell under Palus\'s sway. It is my deduction she operated
disguised as Jannath/\"The Ancient Mother,\" a Netherese deity whose
worship they may have brought or been induced to practice. Under this
guise, she guided them to build the hidden shrine (a locus
for *her* influence, marked by her likeness and built according to
Sembian design) and perform actions described as \"profaning the Waters
and the Grove.\" I deduced the brilliance here: these actions *did* lift
the settlers\' curse by harnessing Thistle Ridge\'s inherent vitality,
BUT simultaneously weakened Palus\'s sister Quercina (guardian of the
Grove) and disrupted the natural order, perfectly serving both Palus\'s
personal vendetta and Netheril\'s aims of destabilizing the Dale. The
settlers achieved their aim, blind to the true cost.

4\. **Escalation & The Tragedy:** Having served their purpose --
establishing a disruptive presence and weakening local protections --
the settlers became expendable. The 1439 sacrifice of the false
Alavandors (Andarl, Abrecia, Dorian), orchestrated by Palus acting as
\"The Ancient Mother\" (as Abrecia\'s spirit attested), was the prelude,
proving the method and the settlers\' disposability. By 1441, Palus
initiated the endgame. The sacrifice of the three girls (Lilly
Clarandal, Lilac Alavandor, Poppy Sweetberry) activated the *Tenebrius
Ostium* -- sophisticated Netherese artifacts designed to create
Shadowfell beachheads (a primary Netherese objective, linked directly
back to methods employed in Daerlun as attested in Winters\' insightful
report). This act simultaneously cemented Palus\'s power, fueled by the
captured ghosts, and obliterated Quercina\'s legacy (symbolized by the
destruction of the great oak, Harngorn).

**Phase 2: Palus\'s Consolidation & Potential Betrayal (Post-1441 DR)**

5\. **An Empowered Hag:** The 1441 Tragedy left Palus significantly
empowered, drawing strength from the ghosts now bound to her and the
ambient Shadowfell energy leaking from the active *Ostia*.

6\. **The Turning Point (My Deduction):** Herein lies a crucial
inference. Why did Netheril not consolidate direct control after 1441?
The most logical explanation, obvious to a keen observer, is that Palus,
having achieved her aims and brimming with newfound power, turned on her
erstwhile allies. Driven by her own inscrutable agenda -- whether
consolidating a Fey domain tainted by Shadow or perhaps just sheer
malice -- she likely secured Thistle Ridge *for herself*, becoming an
obstacle rather than an asset to Netheril. This explains the 40-year
stalemate and Netheril\'s lack of direct occupation. Palus, the agent,
became the new problem.

**Phase 3: The Current Situation & the Reclamation Effort (1483 DR)**

7\. **Palus as the Immediate Obstacle:** If my deduction holds, Palus
and her goblin thralls (the Yellow Tooth clan, led by her likely
subordinate, Charrah) are now the primary impediment
to *anyone* controlling Thistle Ridge -- including Netheril.

8\. **Netheril\'s New Game (Are We Being Played?):** This reframes the
entire Reclamation effort. Could Netheril be subtly allowing, even
encouraging, the Governors' Assembly to mount the campaign to reclaim
Thistle Ridge? If so, Chrysalis has become their unwitting tool to
remove the inconveniently empowered Palus. Once we succeed (as we
inevitably shall, under our Governess's competent leadership, naturally
guided by my own advice), Netheril can then move against a weakened
victor to seize the prize. A complex, layered deception entirely in
keeping with their methods.

9\. **Interpreting Current Hostility:** Netheril\'s recent overt attacks
-- the Purple Worm delivered via the *Orbis Imperiosus* (an artifact
itself likely powered by sacrifices to a Slave Orb, perhaps the very
\"terrible orb\" Jendwendle described Charrah using), the Shadovar
assassins encountered at the temple -- fit this new paradigm. They might
be attempting to weaken *us*, the likely victors over Palus, to ensure
an easier final conquest. Or perhaps they are simply sowing chaos while
the main players (Chrysalis and Palus) are occupied.

**Supporting Circumstantial Evidence (Elaborated for Clarity):**

**Netherese Patterns:** The established pattern of Netherese
interference in the Dale (supporting the Shar-worshipping Dark Garden,
the brutal Sons of Bel) demonstrates their preference for using local
conflicts and agents to achieve their aims, mirroring their likely use
of Palus and the cursed settlers.

**The Hidden Shrine:** The secrecy surrounding this structure, its
Sembian design pointing to the impostor settlers, and its likely
dedication to a Netherese entity (Jannath) under Palus\'s influence, all
scream covert operation. It served as a tool for manipulation, not a
primary objective in itself.

**Sacrificial Precedent:** The 1439 sacrifice, hushed up as a hunting
accident and blamed on \"The Ancient Mother\" (Palus\'s likely guise),
clearly establishes the methodology of ritual death via trickery years
before the 1441 Tragedy.

**Tergus\'s Doubts:** The journal entries of Tergus Clarandal (a member
of the impostor lineage) reveal his own suspicions about their claimed
Sembian origins (the Daerlun/Saerloon discrepancy) and a sense of
\"hidden truth,\" providing invaluable internal corroboration that their
identity was fabricated.

**Information Control:** The presence of figures like Magistrate
Invictus, noted by Tergus as a \"keeper of secrets,\" suggests potential
mechanisms for suppressing inconvenient truths that might expose the
conspiracy.

**The Pattern of Threes:** The recurrence of three sacrificial victims
in each major event (1439: Andarl, Abrecia, Dorian; 1441: Lilly, Lilac,
Poppy; unknown year: Palus\'s Kin: Daughter, Granddaughter, Palus
herself symbolically) hints at a ritualistic significance demanding
further investigation. *(Note to Self: Research Netherese and Hag
numerology. Is \'three times three\' required for activating specific
artifacts or pacts?)*

**Symbolic Congruence:** The alignment between the sacrificed girls\'
families and the locations of the Shadowfell infestations is too precise
to be coincidental. Lilac, bearing the false **Alavandor** name, links
to the **Alavandor Academy**. Poppy **Sweetberry**, whose family ran
the **Oak and Shield Inn** (as noted in the 1440 survey), links to that
establishment. Lilly **Clarandal**, from the family involved in
governance and trade, plausibly links to the **Merchants\' Hall**. This
points to Palus\'s deliberate, almost theatrical, cruelty in placing
these specific hauntings.

**Conclusion:** *The Thistle Ridge affair is a multi-generational
conspiracy. Netheril initiated it, using cursed impostors managed by a
pre-corrupted Palus. Palus manipulated them to lift their curse while
destroying her sister\'s influence and activating Netherese artifacts.
Empowered, she likely betrayed Netheril, seizing control for herself.
Now, Netheril may be subtly using Daggerdale\'s Reclamation (and our
company) to remove Palus, planning to claim the spoils afterward. A
tangled web, indeed, but one I have now clearly mapped. No one outwits a
true Alavandor!*

**Outstanding Questions (Trivialities Requiring Final Confirmation):**

- The precise role of the Alavaxi. *(Likely another Netherese vector,
  perhaps intended as a backup or failsafe, explaining Alaventa\'s
  unique status).*

- The exact mechanics and number of the *Tenebrius Ostia*. *(Netherese
  artifacts activated by sacrifice -- the core function is clear. Were
  they supplied or locally produced? The former seems most likely)*

- The definitive relationship between Palus and Jannath/\"Ancient
  Mother.\" *(Almost certainly Palus operating under a convenient guise
  to manipulate those predisposed to Netherese beliefs).*

- Palus\'s ultimate loyalty. *(To herself, undoubtedly. Alliances were
  merely tools for her ancient feud and power).*

- Understanding this full scope -- the initial manipulation, the likely
  betrayal, and the current multi-layered conflict -- is essential for
  navigating the treacherous path ahead. Forewarned, as they say, is
  forearmed, especially when guided by an intellect capable of seeing
  the entire board.

Lucan Alavandor
