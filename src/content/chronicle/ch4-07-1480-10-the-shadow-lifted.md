## Preface

In midsummer of 1479 DR, the Order learned of a great struggle in the
distant land of Daggerdale.  The nature of this struggle was, on the
surface, that of two clashing polities; the powerful Empire of Netheril,
and the small but tenacious Daggerdale.  Daggerdale has checked the
advance of its ruthless and imperialistic neighbour since the Spell
Plague, and today continues to exemplify the triumph of the small and
righteous over the great and wicked.  However, a divine struggle was at
the core of this conflict, and it was this aspect of the struggle which
brought me to Daggerdale, in the autumn of 1479.  I immediately made
contact with Magistrate Hoetmer, as instructed.  This kind man was
already known to us and was inextricably woven into this conflict.

The seasons I spent in the Historic Wing of the Yellow Abbey before the
summer of 1480, and the manner in which I researched the struggle and
looked for solutions to the myriad threats to the people of Daggerdale
is well documented already in my report from Kythorn 4, 1480.  However,
I will summarize it neatly, herein.

Daggerdale is run by an amazing and accomplished bureaucracy that is
robust, if not a bit daunting.  They employ a system of messages and
intelligence that I do not believe is matched in their part of the
world.  The volume of information they generate is unbelievable, and to
criticize their system just a little, I must say that they were unable
to process it all effectively.  This is where my talents were offered
and properly employed by the Bishop of Dagger Falls, Blaze Whitegate.

On the eve of the Saint's Battle, there were two major fronts in the war
with Netheril.  The more overtly military front was on the western
border of the dale, where forces were arrayed for a full-scale war.  The
other front was in the heartland of the dale, where a trio of agents of
Netheril, now known as the Dark Garden, worked to cause strife and
discord within the population of the dale.  It was on this front that my
skills were employed.  The Dark Garden also used a system of
intelligence and complicated ruses to accomplish their task, which was
to instigate powerful cults of Shar in the Dale.  My previous report
summarizes the relationship between the Dale and Shar, and I shall not
delve too deeply into this convoluted and storied relationship here. 
Suffice it to say that there was fertile ground for these covens to be
established, and the government in the Dale could not get ahead of these
clever villainesses. 

The White Cloaks (refer to my last report for details about this
organization) took on the task of hunting the Dark Garden.  There were
many capable men and women in their ranks, and the thumb-screw methods
of the zealot Alystair Dretch were enough to uncover the least of the
established covens.  I have much to say about Mr. Dretch, but I will
simply state here that this man got results when no one else was able to
pin down any operation of the Dark Garden.  His cooperation with
Magistrate Hoetmer was instrumental in setting into motion a series of
events that culminated in the complete extermination of the Dark Garden,
and the triumph of the Dale over Netheril. 

For my part, I dissected the voluminous stacks of reports that came
daily to the Tower, and acting upon intelligence gathered by the White
Cloak team lead by Iolus Warburton (more on this man, later in this
report), was able to locate one of the major covens established by the
Dark Garden.  I was also able to help the enigmatic Magistrate Hoetmer
with his Aetherial Gift (more on this later), which in return helped in
the final reconnaissance and siege upon Liberty Hill Farm.

Such was my role; gatherer of knowledge, and advisor.  There have been
many days since the 1st of Kythorn 1480 that I have regretted this role,
for I played a part in sending many men and women off to kill and to
die.  However, in the end it brought a peace to the Dale that has not
been seen in over 100 years, and in the end, I gained a few friends so
close that to be away from them now, cloistered here in Waterdeep, is a
pain almost beyond enduring.

This report will summarize the political and religious situation in
Daggerdale on the eve of the Saint's Battle.  It will provide a brief
account of the major parties involved in the conflict.  The Saint's
Battle itself will not be dealt with in much detail by my pen; I am
including a dozen accounts of this battle by credible chroniclers who
fought there, and my contribution cannot match theirs; I was not at that
Battle.

The focus of this report will be on the events that unfolded after the
Battle, and how peace came to the dale, and how a devastated community
was not only rebuilt, but grew beyond what it was before the Shadow fell
upon it.

I will state presently that this is not an unbiased account.  The
Canoness is aware that I became emotionally involved in this assignment,
despite her warnings.  Indeed, I fell in love with Magistrate Hoetmer. 
The death of my Phin still brings me to tears, even though I have
received the switch several times for my conspicuous disregard of my
duty to remain emotionless.  It is well known by the sisterhood that I
begged for an extension of this assignment, and that I stayed on a year
after the Battle.  However, it is not known that Phin was not the only
reason I stayed on; unfortunately, he died shortly after the Battle, and
it was the friendship of Troia Trustar and Lumina Whitegate that kept me
there.  I very nearly did not return from Liberty Hill.  My mind is, as
ever, focused on the Order and my duties, but my heart remains with
those two women, inside the white stone walls of that happy place.

As per the Directives of the Order, the original copy of this manuscript
has been left in-situ; it resides in the Historic Wing of the Yellow
Abbey.  I happily submit this copy to the Order, to be enshrined in the
Font of Knowledge among much greater works.

## The Shadow's Reach:  The Situation in Daggerdale on the Eve of the Saint's Battle

*The Western Front*

Towards the end of the harvest season of 1479, Netherese forces in the
plains west of the mountains began to mount.  Increased numbers of
skirmishes between reconnaissance elements of both Netheril and
Daggerdale were reported starting after Harvestide, and persisted until
the spring of 1480. 

Before the snows had fully receded in the middle of Ches, large forces
of Netherese Regulars were seen patrolling just a dozen miles away from
both major forts on the border, Lastsun and Sansum.  By the beginning of
Tarsakh, division-strength Netherese formations were sighted a day away
from each fort.  Fortunately, the scouting conflicts of the winter were
interpreted properly; Lord Whitegate had already authorized a massive
military expansion program which included training new companies of Sun
Blades, keeping veterans on for a longer term (with a favourable pay),
and the hiring of mercenary outfits, some at battalion-strength.

The mount-up to this conflict drained the heretofore bottomless coffers
of the Whitegate fortune; there were more mercenaries to pay, and
sensing the dire need of the good people of Daggerdale, they duly raised
their prices.  Mercenaries are a necessary evil in such times, I
suppose.  Still, it is confirmed that the footmen in the mercenary
battalion known as the Red Jacks were paid 2 gold coins per day to be
stationed at Fort Lastsun, which is an unheard-of income for a soldier.

Most of the headquarters-units were stationed on the western front.  The
upper command of the Sun Blades, General Thunderstorm's company, was
stationed at Lastsun, and most of the upper command of the Order of the
Lambent Shield, Luminous included, were stationed at both forts, and saw
combat almost daily throughout the summer.

It is the opinion of this sister of the Order of the Tome that the
military manouverings in the west were an elaborate distraction from the
more proximal threat to the dale, which was the effort of the Dark
Garden in the heartland.  So long as there were divisions of Netherese
regulars threatening the front, troops could not be spared to help in
the heartland, and little money was available to hire adventurers to
root out the Dark Garden.  Fortunately, the White Cloaks took on this
responsibility themselves, at their own expense (though they were
handsomely rewarded in the end).

*The Heartland*

In the fall of 1479, Alystair Dretch and Tuugan Swift uncovered the
least of three powerful covens established by the Dark Garden.  They
managed to destroy it utterly before it could take a firm footing in the
dale, which is fortunate.  Their efforts put them in touch with
Magistrate Hoetmer, who was already tied to this struggle on some
obscure divine level before the White Cloaks even arrived.

In the spring of 1480, the efforts of the Dark Garden became bolder, and
their tactics changed dramatically.  Having established two covens in
the dale through their intricate plotting and devious and protracted
plans, they now exploited the situation in the west.  Before the snows
were gone, nearly every farm found itself in some sort of danger; the
usual problems of wolves, orcs, goblins and other common threats were
increased dramatically, illustrating the broad reach of the Dark
Garden.  They had many tribes of orcs and other monsters under their
influence, and even controlled many of the baser and meaner creatures of
the woods through the villainess known as Deadnettle (a black druid of
some variety).  At the very time that more men were needed to face
Netheril in the west, these problems erupted in the heartland.  The
threat to each farm was such that none could help another.  This was the
plan all along; the Dark Garden wanted to isolate the farms where they
had established their covens, so that they would have the time to carry
out exceptionally sinister rituals to enslave the entire populations of
each farm, and also employ many companies of undead.

With all military resources committed to either defending the western
front or defending hearth and home, all that remained to ferret out the
Dark Garden was the White Cloaks.  They did this, at great cost to
themselves.  Iolus' team of White Cloaks managed to end a significant
threat to one farm, Tesh Valley Farm, in the early spring.  They
uncovered a plot by Deadnettle which involved a Trollspawn (which we
haven't heard of in nearly a century) and a black dragon known to us;
Kruhwahll.  This monster was lured from its home far to the north by
Deadnettle in the winter.  She used deception to control both it and the
sinister breed of trolls that were spawned from the eggs of the dragon
and the Trollspawn.  I have submitted a detailed report on this topic
already.

*The Dagger Hills*

This region is also worth noting, because it is tied to the Dark Garden
and the efforts of Netheril, though I am not now sure that there is a
direct correlation, but rather one of degrees.

The opening to the Shadowfell that was discovered by the White Cloaks in
the summer of 1479 factors into this report significantly.  It is known
that the Dark Garden went in and out of this opening a number of times,
and they likely lead many evil creatures from the Shadowfell, into our
world.  These creatures disrupted the balance of power in the Dagger
Hills in the 1479 (see my report about the White Cloaks' mission there,
and c.f. Braxus, Silver Tail Clan), and by 1480, the hills were abroil
in conflict which spread into some of the neighbouring farms.  The
shadow-things that crept through the hills made conflicts with some of
the native population of were-creatures, enslaved some, and allied with
yet others.  Despite the hasty construction of the new fort,
appropriately named the Shadow Dam, over that portal, and the allocation
of a good portion of the Order of the Lambent Shield and a new,
specially trained company of Sun Blades, the trouble in the hills could
not be contained.

It is the speculation of this sister that the opening to the Shadowfell
under the Shadow Dam, in the back of the cave previously inhabited by
the benign werewolf Braxus, was the creation of the leader of the Dark
Garden, the priestess of Shar known as Belladonna.  I will go into
detail about her and her relationship with Castle Daggerdale below, but
I will state here that it is likely that she tried to create an opening
from the Shadowfell leading into the fearsome castle in the hills, but
that the ritual went slightly wrong, creating the opening a few miles
away by mistake, and leading to the Shadowfell Exchange in the Dagger
Hills.

*The White Cloaks*

Through the hospitality of White Cloak Tower at Castle Radiance, this
organization grew tremendously in the winter of 1480, the hostel that it
operated out of the ground floor of the tower working as an effective
recruitment tool.  By the time conflicts started to spark up like so
many forest fires around the farms of the dale, the White Cloaks had
three full teams of adventurers to start hunting down the Dark Garden
and to try to stem the flow of conflict in the heartland.  As stated
previously and reported on in-full, the senior team, that lead by Iolus
Warburton, was able to identify and drive off one member of the Dark
Garden, Deadnettle, and uncovered evidence which linked the Dark Garden,
although tenuously, to the command structure of Netheril, through the
Shade Prince Melagaunt, and his Curator, Loki Mouvranesh.  Ironically,
many of the new recruits to the White Cloaks had run into Loki the
summer previous, which ended badly for them.  The results of their
failed mission and the staggering repercussions would be seen a year
later, at the Saints' Battle.

By this time, Bannit was bearing the relic of Ardelusk Whitegate, the
Blade of the White Saint, and his results with that blade in hand are
beyond reproach. 

It should be noted that many of the White Cloaks suffered from the same
dreams of the Dark Garden (in the actual horticultural sense\...more on
this term below) that plagued Magistrate Hoetmer.  Their connection was
not fully understood at the time, and is only theoretically and
tenuously explained now, but this connection helped Magistrate Hoetmer
to alert select White Cloaks when they were in trouble.   This
connection alone explains the escape from the trap laid for the White
Cloaks at Liberty Hill Farm\...

## The Heart of the Shadow, the Heart of the Dale

The Dark Garden successfully established two covens in the autumn of
1479.  The coven at Dwimmerholme Farm, which was to be established by
the Dark Garden agent known as Lotus, was foiled by Alystair and
Tuugan.  However, two were established before that; the lesser of these
was at Foot Hills Farm, and was likely established by Deadnettle, who
was operating around there at the time.  The greater was at Liberty Hill
Farm.

Liberty Hill Farm is the hearth of the Whitegate Family.  I have
included Ardelusk's account of his founding of this settlement nearly a
century ago to give a sense of the central nature of this place to the
prominent family of the dale.  This farm had always been exceptionally
tolerant of other faiths, despite the strong investment that the
Whitegate family has in Amaunator, and it was the proliferation and
strength of the Chauntean faith here which was exploited by the Dark
Garden.  In fact, this was the common 'weakness' (I am loathe to label
it such) for all three farms; they were tolerant of, and had large
Chauntean flocks.  The 'Old Religion' (as they call Chauntea in the
Dales) was used as an inroad for the Sharran cults, and where Chauntea
was strong, so too was the influence of the Dark Garden.

I will confess that I felt like I had just graduated from rhetoric
school when these facts came to light.  The term 'Dark Garden' carries
with it something of a horticultural suggestion, which should have
alerted me and others to the divine connection and conflict here in the
dale.  However, no one made this connection until after these events
came to a head.

On the eve of the battle, Liberty Hill Farm had become the Heart of the
Shadow.  The Dark Garden members had all collected there, and elaborate
rituals were used to turn the population of the town into malign and
shadowy versions of themselves.  There was some resistance to this evil
plot as it unfolded, and some of the people of the farm realized what
was happening and fought back. 

Here is a reconstruction of the events at the Farm, as I interpret them;

> 1372:  The real history and the events leading up to the battle begin
> with the Battle of Liberty Hill, in the distant past.  Both Ardelusk
> and the woman known as Arabella Needle fought in this battle, and
> their descendants ordered the battle that took place there in 1480,
> though they were now on opposite sides of the same conflict. 
>
> 1374:  Needle, now going by the name 'Liberty', and with a boy-child
> in tow, visited the farm that Ardelusk had started, and encouraged him
> to name the farm Liberty Hill.  The boy was none other than Gaebril
> Tremalkin-Liberty, the son of the famous Knight of Shar and the Needle
> woman.  Unfortunately, Needle had already taken the darker path
> offered by Shar in those days, misinterpreting the message of the
> Black Knight.  Needle's line was prolific, and many of her
> descendants, through the Tremalkin boy, still live, including
> Belladonna herself.
>
> 1395: Ardelusk moves the Cenotaph of Paladin's Grave, and the Knights
> interred therein, from Anathar's Dell to their new resting place on
> the western side of the town at Liberty Hill.  These same Knights
> would be raised by the wicked Belladonna in the spring of 1480 to
> assist in the battle.
>
> 1455: This was a pivotal year for a number of reasons.  First, the
> Whitegate family (the most significant family of Amaunatorians) was
> joined with the Forester family (the most significant family of
> Chaunteans) in the union of Perspicuus and Lanya.  This brought
> Liberty Hill into the Chauntean fold, which increased the health and
> wealth of the farm tremendously, but also provided an in-road for the
> Dark Garden.  The wedding, on the 1st of Kythorn, turned into a
> massacre, as Dorian of Sembia returned from the dead, seemingly as
> some vampire or revenant, to wreak havoc and death at the ceremony. 
> The account given by Decker Blackmane, which we have had for many
> years, is close, but it misses the mark.  Dorian was not in charge of
> the forces of undead that fought in the fields, taking the lives of
> Loedegan and Madigan.  It was in fact Belladonna who mustered those
> ranks.  She in fact it was that summoned Dorian himself to this
> massacre.

                At the time Belladonna lived at Crossed Blades Farm
(though she spent much of her time at Castle Daggerdale), with her
daughters.  She engineered the massacre as a means of elevating the
station of the Chaunteans at the farm, and she was largely successful. 
She or her agents were able to convince the people of the farm and the
administration of the Church that the malevolent powers opposed to the
Dale recognized that the marriage of the Whitegates and Foresters, of
Amaunator with Chauntea, would be catastrophic for their evil plots. 
So, an army of undead was arrayed to end the wedding, but the assault
failed.  Belladonna was at the wedding ceremony, and it was she that
incapacitated Dorian, her minion, just as Argent attacked it. 

                I have not told a soul in the dale this last fact, as it
would detract from the authority and esteem of the Bishop, and this fact
alone has been kept out of the original manuscript of this report, Oghma
forgive me.

                But I must continue with 1455; Belladonna installed one
of her daughters in the town at Liberty Hill that year; the Chauntean
(in public, but Sharran behind closed doors) Remalla Goering (Goering
was a man that Belladonna had paired with in Crossed Blades, but who
died mysteriously).  Remalla was only 15 years old, but she was a good
friend of Lanya's, and so she was raised as a sister to this now
well-bred woman.  She would grow in prosperity and power at Liberty
Hill, ultimately becoming the elected mayor in 1477, when Lumen
Whitegate abdicated governance of the farm to an elected official.

                Also in 1455, construction of the Table of the Sun was
started and completed.  The Table was originally designed to carry out
an elaborate ritual, divinely delivered to Argent, to contain the evil
of Castle Daggerdale.  This ritual has been ongoing since the late
autumn of that year, though its location was moved to the Yellow Abbey
sub-basement in 1457, mostly to get out of the elements.  I have asked
Argent about this ritual, but it is the one secret he will not
relinquish.  He has only stated that "the sacrifice of the clerics that
maintain the ritual, giving up entire months of their lives to chant
continuously, is nothing compared to the sacrifices made by the men who
made the ritual possible" and will state no more.

> 1470's:  Sometime late in this decade, Belladonna assembled the Dark
> Garden, and began a tenuous relationship with Netheril, through the
> Agent known as Curator Loki Mouvranesh.  They began to organize
> banditry and other small crimes in the dale, slowly escalating to the
> plots and events of 1479/80.  The exact nature of the relationship
> between Netheril and the Dark Garden is not fully understood, though
> it seems it was not one of a clear chain of command.
>
> Mirtul, 1479: The group of adventurers known as Mule Company, some of
> which would later be rolled into the ranks of the White Cloaks,
> attempt to claim a bounty (c.f. Last Sands of Anauroch bounty.)  They
> run afoul Mouvranesh, who had beaten them to the Netherese ruin, and
> had already begun an excavation.  She uncovered the Imperiosus Orbis,
> which she used to command the winds to hasten excavation of the site. 
> Mule Company was unable to defeat her, and was driven off after the
> death of one of their members.  Though it was not apparent at the
> time, this was a turning point in the war with Netheril; the powerful
> Netherese Orbs were used to bolster a number of malevolent rituals
> carried out by the Shades, including the rituals that transformed
> Liberty Hill (see below).
>
> Kythorn 10, 1479: Summer Solstice and new moon.  This was the first
> successful Ritual of Founding that the Dark Garden achieved.  This was
> the first we missed, though we knew, after the events of Harvestide
> that year (c.f. Investigations of Alystair Dretch, 1479) that we had
> missed something.  This was the culmination of decades of inculcation
> of the cult of Shar at Liberty Hill, masterminded by Mayor Goering,
> with the help of her mother and the Dark Garden.  It now seems that as
> many as 140 people were part of this cult and the ritual. 
>
> Early Spring, 1480: Belladonna begins to assemble an undead horde,
> unearthing the evil paladins and soldiers interred at Anathar's Dell,
> a place that very few could now find. 
>
> Spring, 1480: The conflict on the Western Front escalates to such a
> degree that no man can be spared; the heartland, despite being under
> an increased level of threats, most of which have been engineered by
> the Dark Garden, has no additional forces to aid the struggle against
> the villainous trio.
>
> Late Kythorn or Early Mirtul, 1480: Belladonna is given the Slave Orbs
> for the Imperiosus Orbis, which will facilitate the exceptionally
> powerful rituals in the days to come.   The Slave Orbs allow for the
> channelling of the tremendous power of the Orbis to a remote location.
>
> Mirtul 19, 1480: New moon.  I believe this is the date that the Ritual
> of Transcendence was completed, with the aid of the Slave Orbs,
> turning much of the town into the shadow slaves that were later fought
> at the Battle.  At this point, the entire farm was under complete
> control of the cult of Shar, and the Dark Garden.  Goering, now a
> shadow-kind and priestess of Shar, was the ruler of the town in its
> new hellish form, though Belladonna was the head of the cult in the
> region.  All but pre-pubescent children were converted into shadow
> slaves.  The children were kept in a barn, and were slated to be sold
> to the Bloody Eye, a slaving and bandit outfit that the Dark Garden
> had employed/manipulated for nearly a decade.  This never happened,
> thankfully.

                The 'transcendence' did not go smoothly.  There was a
resistance mounted against this ritual, and the entire town was thrown
into a violent massacre of brother against brother.  The terrible
stories of this black day continue to be told by its survivors, and each
new story brings a river of tears.

                Nearly a hundred adults in the farm seemed to have the
same resistance to the powerful transcendence ritual as did the
children; this included the entire clergy of Amaunatorians, as well as
many in the Whitegate family.  Those that resisted but were not killed
in the massacre, were kept alive for an even fouler purpose a few days
later.

> Mirtul 19-21, 1480: Belladonna unearths the bodies of the Fist
> mercenaries, interred beneath the three great cairns on the edge of
> the farm, using the labour of the shadow slaves.
>
> Mirtul 21, 1480: Ritual of Corruption begins.  The summoning of the
> Soul of Corruption is complete, again with the aid of the Slave Orbs,
> but control of the thing is tenuous; a host of clerics chant at all
> times to maintain control of it.  The Soul of Corruption is known to
> the Order, but I believe I am the first to have any involvement so
> close with the thing.  It was likely employed to keep people both in
> and out of the farm.

                The Ritual of Corruption needed not only the bolstering
might of the Slave Orbs, but a *constant supply of fresh blood*, which
was grotesquely provided by the prisoners that resisted the
transcendence.  These unfortunate souls were eviscerated and then hung,
still living but in unthinkable agony, from the walls of the Table of
the Sun.  Their eviscerations and hangings were expertly carried out to
leave them living for as long as possible; so long as blood dripped from
their still-living bodies, the Ritual of Corruption could be
maintained.  In this horrifying and gruesome fashion, every member of
the Whitegate family that resided at the farm perished, as did
Magistrate Zuldrick.  Their corpses were found decorating the walls by
those that survived the Battle.

> Mirtul 23, 1480: Alystair Dretch and Tuugan Swift attempt to
> investigate the farm, based on their own research (Hoetmer and I had
> arrived at the conclusion that this investigation was necessary at the
> same time, but to their credit, the White Cloaks were a step ahead of
> us).  The pair of able White Cloaks were rebuked by the Soul of
> Corruption, which met them on the road and drove them off.  They had
> fought through throngs of orcs to get that far, but their
> determination was no match for this new terror.
>
> Mirtul 25, 1480: Alystair and Tuugan return to Dagger Falls, and link
> up with many of their fellow White Cloaks.  The group of them met with
> Hoetmer and myself, and we discussed what we thought was going on at
> the farm.  We were not fully aware of what the situation might be, and
> unanimously decided that another team had to attempt to get into the
> farm to see what was going on.  Alystair, Tuugan, Alen Reks, Ganolen
> Thendolain, and Troia Trustar were formed into a team and headed out
> to investigate immediately.
>

## Immediate Aftermath of the Saints' Battle

The Saint's Battle came to its conclusion by fits and starts.  When the
White Cloaks were able to infiltrate the cathedral and interrupt the
Ritual of Corruption, it is widely reported that an audible cheer was
heard over the entire battlefield, as the Soul of Corruption ceased its
merciless assault on the Army of the White Saint, and dissipated
harmlessly.  The first company over the wall was the 2nd Dalehearth
Platoon, and they gained the wall after driving off Lotus.  Their
banners flying on the walls inspired many other companies to rally and
redouble their efforts at the hopeless siege.  Finally, when the White
Cloaks gained the Table of the Sun, fought with Belladonna and her
guardian, and ultimately defeated her through the courageous action of
Drake Sterling, the battle was over.

By the time Drake and his captive hit the ground (he actually grabbed
Belladonna and plummeted over the side of the tower with her!), there
were already 3 platoons of Sun Blades, a small host of Knights, and
Bishop Whitegate at the base of the tower; they had fought their way up
the street, and the sight of the flashing Blade of the White Saint on
the battlements of the Table of the Sun inspired them to victory.  So it
was that as Drake hit the ground and lost consciousness, there were men
on hand to apprehend the villainess that had masterminded this terrible
plot.  Belladonna was taken into custody by Bishop Whitegate, and Drake
was taken to the medical tents.

The Battle was over; Belladonna was defeated, the malign Rituals that
had transformed the people and the place were disrupted.  Most of the
undead had been destroyed, and the human victims of the Ritual of
Transcendence who had been turned into shadow-fiend versions of
themselves, slowly came to their senses.  Those few that were loyal to
the cause before that terrible ritual were already dead or captured.

It was discovered that Lotus, seeing that her troops were going to lose
the wall once the Soul of Corruption was taken out of play, attempted to
flee.  However, here a card was played by my Phin.  We had scrutinized
every report about the Dark Garden we had available to us, a score of
times or more.  We knew that those women could all shape-shift into
ravens, and that their apprehension would be nearly impossible.  We also
knew about the deal that Warburton had made with Kruhwahll.  Phin was
able to contact the dragon, through means that he would not divulge to
me, and made another deal with it.  He said he would tell the dragon
where the Dark Garden could be found, if it kept out of sight of the men
of the battle, and if it left the Warburton farm.  The dragon kept to
the deal, for the most part; when Lotus tried to fly away, Kruhawahll
swept down to consume her.  There are rumours of a dragon in the dark
skies above the battle, but there is no one who will go on the record to
say that they saw such a dragon.  Of his agreement to leave Warburton
farm, that must be dealt with in another report.

Deadnettle was also killed in the battle.  As the Whitecloaks fought
their way up the citadel, trying to get to the Table of the Sun on the
battlements, they were attacked by Deadnettle and a host of her Black
Trolls.  In this encounter, she was slain outright.

So, the Dark Garden was entirely defeated, as was the Army of the Black
Saint.  The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin was in the possession of the
Bishop.  Liberty Hill Farm was secure. 

However, the pain and suffering were just beginning.

Before the Dark Garden took over the farm, and enacted its many malign
rituals, the population of the farm was over 800.  As the sun set on the
1st of Kythorn, 1480, the population of the farm was 225.  Of those,
most were children; only a handful of adults had survived the Ritual of
Transcendence and the bloody siege.  The entire organizational structure
of the farm had been completely eliminated; there was not a single good
priest or priestess left in town, nor were there any elders or military
leaders.

The state of the farm itself was terrible; the slaughter before the
siege and the siege itself left hundreds dead on both sides, fires and
siege weapons had demolished about a fifth of the buildings, and the
fields were black from fires and necromantic infection.  It was a
disheartening sight for all; bodies were strewn about the streets,
including the ghastly sight of the mutilated undead everywhere.  From
the walls of the central citadel were hung almost 30 citizens, including
every Whitegate and elder in the village; they had been left to bleed
out slowly to maintain the Ritual of Corruption, and the horror and pain
on their dead faces and the gruesome eviscerations that dangled many
feet below them was too much to witness, especially for their family
members.  Even worse; some were yet alive, and though Bannitt insisted
that they could be saved, none of them wanted to live after the ordeal. 
The final details of their lives are best kept off the record.

Nearly every child in the village had become an orphan.  Some of the
suckling children had perished for want of their mothers, and there are
a few terrible stories about some unfortunate children that my heart
can't bear to report.  But, it is a small blessing that most of the
children did indeed survive.   As it happens, the children were spared
by Belladonna because she had made arrangements with the Bloody Eye to
sell them into slavery.  We learned this from Malveau, who showed up a
day after the battle, who had followed these bandits back into the dale.

Added to the misery of the raw carnage, hitting the survivors and the
troops that liberated the farm shortly after the euphoria of victory was
diminished, was the guilt that nearly every soldier and citizen felt at
about having to battle their countrymen.  There are numerous tales of
soldiers having killed their shadow-enslaved relatives, then falling
completely apart emotionally only a few moments after Belladonna was in
custody.  Again, there are countless tales to tear the heart from one's
chest, and they will not be recounted here.  Suffice it to say, there
was no one glad to have been involved in this endeavour, no matter what
side they fought on.

Hardest hit, though, was the Whitegate Family.  Lumina, who had been
struggling with her own experiences facing off against her enemies, was
a shell for a long time after the battle.  I am told that she led the
3rd platoon of the Dalehearth Company down the main concourse of the
village as the battle came to a close, and that as soon as she came to
the great courtyard in front of the citadel, and saw the nightmare
hangings thereupon, that she fell to her knees.  I can personally
testify that she said not a word from that point on, until many weeks
later.  We all feared for her soul, and though she recovered it, for the
most part, it remains to this day a cherished treasure to be closely
guarded.

Immediately after the battle had concluded, there was, of course, a
tremendous amount of disorder.  Even with Bishop Whitegate there, there
was so much madness to be resolved that it was many hours before the
wailing, screaming, suffering and general chaos was abated.  During
these tumultuous hours, a few notable events happened.

First, and I have mentioned it; Belladonna was taken prisoner, rather
than being executed on the spot, as nearly every soldier, citizen and
child was screaming for.  The Bishop was every inch the lord of the
place, and with the support of Knight Commander Pauldron, she was kept
alive, and was secured and taken away.  I am unsure where they took her
right away, but I will tell of her fate below.

Second; though the battle with the undead and shadow-slaves of the Army
of the Black Saint had ended, there were still three companies of orcs
engaged with various elements of the Army of the White Saint outside the
walls.  This part of the battle was not going well for the tired men of
the dale, and many might have perished if not for the arrival of Braxus,
the werewolf lord from Gang'Dar.  He swept through the ranks of orcs and
killed and scattered them in little time.  The Sun Blades that he had
delivered were wary of this awesome creature; none would approach him to
give thanks.  Braxus simply waited outside the gates, stalking among the
dead and dying outside, dispatching orcs here and there.  At length,
Troia went out to greet him.  What transpired between them none then
knew.  Braxus left and Troia returned.

Third; some of the Whitecloaks rushed to Lumina when they saw her falter
in the courtyard.  Though she said nothing to any of them, she did one
single act that spoke volumes; she handed the epaulets from her cloak to
Iolus.  All the Whitecloaks hailed Iolus as their new captain.  From
that time on, Lumina had no part in the company she helped to create. 
She has been living a peaceful life at the farm since that day, and
Iolus has been an inspiring captain.

Fourth; Bannitt cut down the body of Magistrate Zuldrick, and was seen
to weep at the death of the man who seemed to have died the cruellest
death of the lot; he was tortured and mutilated beyond recognition, save
for his blood-soaked vestments.  At that moment, Bannitt made an
emotionally charged plea to Bishop Whitegate to be instated as the
Magistrate of Liberty Hill Farm.  For a moment afterwards, all were
silent; it is said that White Cloaks and Knights of the Lambent Shield
alike stared in disbelief, awaiting the reply from the Bishop. 
Whitegate took a moment to consider it, drawing the suspense of the
moment out.  Bannitt made a beautiful speech which I will append to this
report; it is well documented.  His case was that he had come from a
far-off land on pilgrimage to see the White Saint.  He had liberated the
home of the Saint with his very own blade, and felt as though his
journey had come to its end.  The bishop agreed, and Bannitt was
invested on the spot.  He remains the Magistrate at the farm, and a
close friend.

Fifth; the Slave Orbs of the Imperialis Orbis were taken from the Table
of the Sun.  These were indeed brought by the Bishop right to Phin and I
for examination.  They are terrible, powerful artefacts that are tied to
the Imperialis Orbis, which was plundered from the depths of a Netherese
ruin the summer before by Loki.  Ironically, it was Bannitt's team of
bounty-seekers that failed that mission, and it was he that carried the
blade of the White Saint to victory on the Table of the Sun, thus
helping to capture these weapons in the end.  I have appended a report
about the Slave Orbs.

In the days that followed, many stayed at Liberty Hill.  Many soldiers
who had relations there (dead or alive) stayed to help out.  Of the
White Cloaks, Bannitt, Lumina, and Troia stayed on.  Bannitt, of course,
had an office to keep, and Troia stayed mostly to look after her
friend.  For the rest, there was still a war raging, and many loose ends
to wrap up.  With apologies, the rest of the White Cloaks and most of
the army parted the morning after the battle; they were headed off to
Foot Hills Farm to put an end to the last of the covens of Shar.  I have
appended the report from Alystiar Dretch about this encounter, but
suffice it to say, the momentum of this victory was enough to settle
matters at Foot Hills in a relatively bloodless fashion. 

## War Raged On

With the Dark Garden defeated, many of the problems in the Heartland
sorted themselves out in the weeks to come.  Each farm was again able to
secure its borders, and before long, there were once again young men
marching to the Castle, to join the battle on the Western front. 

The forces of Netheril increased the tempo of the war in the west with
the defeat of the Dark Garden.  They no-doubt realized that with this
threat in the heart-land ended, that additional troops would likely be
available to bolster the defences of the Dalesmen on the front.  On the
19th of Flamerule, the definitive battle of the war took place; the
Siege of Fort Lastsun.  I will refer you to the report made by Brother
Neddle of this battle.  It is by all accounts the best compilation and
interpretation of the various reports and stories about the battle.

I must needs summarize it quickly, however, if only to draw a line
between the outcome of that battle and the White Cloaks who unwittingly
contributed so much.

By the 18th of Flamerule, all three divisions of Netherese soldiers
involved in this war were massed a mile away from Fort Lastsun.  It
appeared as though they would make the final surge of the battle;
Lastsun would not be able to endure the assault, even though they were
likely to inflict unthinkable casualties on the Netherese from their
strong defensive position.  We now know that the Obris Imperiosus were
employed for this battle; they used them to darken the skies above the
battle so that the sun never rose on the morning of the 19th; the
Netherese General must have known that time was not on his side.  He
would have waited until the night of the 9th to launch his attack in the
cover of darkness, rather than use the orbs in this capacity and hasten
the attack. 

So, before breakfast was served, the Netherese divisions began their
assault; the valley leading up to the Fort was choked with
twenty-thousand soldiers representing a dozen fiendish races.  Siege
machines launching devilishly-enchanted stones blasted apart the
anterior walls of the fort, and a desperate defence was made by the 1500
men inside.  However, even though they were slaughtering the attackers
at a ratio of 5:1, they would not win the day.  But, help unlooked-for
arrived.

As the men of Fort Lastsun were down to about 300 defenders, and it
seemed as though they would be overrun at any moment, the very earth
shook, and the floor of the valley, and the thousands of attackers
queued to the slaughter thereupon, vanished. The earth opened up and
swallowed them, leaving a deep chasm in front of the ruined Fort.  There
was panic in the Netherese ranks, and those that remained in the castle,
ahead of the army-swallowing chasm, attempted to flee.

To the rear of the chasm, where the Netherese Command headquarters had
been smugly watching the slaughter, another great slaughter happened. 
This time, it was at the hands of the Tehyamar League, which issued out
of the chasm proximal to the Netherese Command; 600 dwarven warriors
emerged to engage double their number of elite headquarters guard.  They
exploited the confusion that their trap had caused, and in the end they
slaughtered and drove off the Command, though nearly 300 of them were
killed in the assault.

The Tethyamar League had rallied dwarves across Northern Faerun in the
months leading up to the war, after the announcement of the discovery of
the Eastern Gate of Tethyamar.  This gate had been discovered by
Deadnettle, who was in turn found by Warburton's team of White Cloaks
months before.  This discovery acted as a lightening rod that attracted
hundreds of dwarves to the region, who were convinced by Curator
Gambledin of the Tethyamar League to do what they could for the war with
Netheril.  Their clever trap, the sapping of an entire valley just in
the knick of time, and their selfless assault upon overwhelming odds
upon the command units, ended the war with Netheril.  The League had
kept their subterranean arrival in the theatre a secret; Br. Neddle
claims that Gambledin did not trust everyone in the command of the Dale
forces to reveal his plans, or even announce that he had assembled the
host he did. 

So, before Shieldmeet, what Netherese forces remained were in full
retreat, and the war was concluded, for the time being.  The losses were
exceptionally heavy, though; nearly 3000 dalesmen lost their lives in
this conflict, and nearly that number again of mercenaries.  The cost to
Netheril has been conservatively estimated at more than double that
figure.

## The Fate of Belladonna

As I write this, Belladonna is a captive on the Table of the Sun at
Liberty Hill.  She was brought there almost immediately after the
Saint's Battle, along with the Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin.  She was
held there without trial until after Shieldmeet of 1480; Argent and
Luminous wanted to be at the trial.  These men had visited their
ancestral home the day of Shieldmeet (a day I shall never forget as long
as I live; I have never witnessed or experienced such peaks and valleys
of emotion as that visit inspired), and soon after rushed to Dagger
Falls to conduct the trial.  I have already forwarded the proceedings of
this trial (c.f. Proceedings of the Trial of Belladonna Liberty, Dagger
Falls, 7 Elesias, 1480 DR), which I would recommend to any student of
jurisprudence.  Despite her unparalleled level of villainy and evil, she
was given a fair trial in which no formality, no matter how trivial, was
disregarded.  I personally attended the trial, clutching the hand of my
friend, Lumina, for the several days that it took to arrive at judgement
and sentencing.  Belladonna was found guilty of a list of crimes that
took thirty minutes to cite.  However, she was also acquitted on a
number of charges that took twice that long to cite; so clever were her
machinations that even as a confessed leader of the worst cult of Shar
in history she could not be convicted of all crimes charged her.

When sentencing began, as is customary in the Dalelands, families and
friends of the victims were heard from.  The Whitegates relinquished
their primacy in these depositions; at the outset, the people cried to
hear Lumina and Luminous speak, but they allowed all others who had a
claim to go ahead of them.  This took another 2 days, and I think
everyone who stayed to listen to the extent of sorrow that was spread by
this woman lost a year of their life for the hearing of it.  I will
never be the same after listening to so many weeping mothers and
orphans.

## The Whitegates

Waterdeep, and I should dispense with any notion that there are
hereditary claims to be made for any positions of governance by the
Thunderstorms and Blackmanes; those two families do indeed have such

## The White Cloaks

The White Cloaks were changed dramatically after the Saints Battle.  All
14 active members of the company stormed the walls of Liberty Hill
during that battle, some as a small and elite unit to infiltrate the
fort quickly, others as leaders of platoons of Sun Blades, Militia, and
volunteers.  All survived bodily, though for some, their story with the
White Cloaks ended that day. 

Lumina handed over the company to her friend and colleague (in both
Faith and Arms), Ioulus Warburton.  She has had no active part of the
White Cloaks since that time, though I can personally attest to the fact
that her cloak remains hanging on the back of her door in her little
cottage up at Liberty Hill.

The tale of Magistrate Harbaugh has already been touched upon.  He was
granted office immediately after the battle, by Bishop Whitegate, and
has maintained that office dutifully and to the betterment of the farm
(see 'Liberty Hill Farm', below) ever since.

Tuugan Swift retired from the ranks in the winter of 1480.  He was never
the same after his narrow escape from the farm on the reconnaissance of
Mirtul 26, 1480, though he soldiered on through the Saints Battle and
many other smaller engagements through until the winter.  He was never
able to face Lumina, though, after their excursion into the Shadowfell
the year before.  I am privy to some of Lumina's secrets, and though I
will keep them as promised, I can say that Lumina and Tuugan shared a
terrible experience together in the Darkness; they both carry a great
guilt about what happened in there, and are unable to forgive themselves
for what they did to each other. 

In early Uktar, Tuugan and Alystair concluded some business at Crossed
Blades Farm.  Alystair announced that he would be going to see his
friends at Liberty Hill, but Tuugan declined to accompany him.  His
reasons were two-fold; he could not face Lumina, but he had also become
involved with a young lady named Milli Oakdale.  This devout Chauntean
(indeed, daughter of the Cultivar of the farm) is the younger sister of
a man named Brag Oakdale, who Tuugan led over the walls of Liberty
Hill.  Brag had introduced his captain to his sister at the Castle Gate
Inn, and things fell into place for the couple. As I write this, I have
just seen Tuugan, having been to the Castle Gate, and though he is
quiet, he is happy; Milli is due to deliver their first child any day
now, and Tuugan is serving drinks at the Inn to make up for his young
wife, whom he will not allow to work.  Tuugan does not keep much company
besides his new family these days; Alystair periodically drops in, but
Tuugan has not sought out any of his old friends, and has not since the
1st of Kythorn, 1480, set foot within the walls of Liberty Hill. 
Alystair tells me, in confidence, that Tuugan has spent many nights
patrolling the dark forest surrounding Liberty Hill Farm, unknown to the
residents he protects.

The White Cloaks continue to operate effectively under the leadership of
Iolus.  The current details of the company, including roster and ranks,
I will append to this report.

What is important to note is that the company was well rewarded for
their efforts in the Saints Battle.  Luminous met with Iolus at the
Yellow Abbey after the sentencing of Belladonna in 1480.  I was on hand
at the time, and was asked to notarize an agreement between these two
men.  Luminous handed over possession of Loedegan Close (the ancestral
home of the Whitegate Family in Town) to the White Cloaks, providing
them with a base of operations within the walls of Town.  The Close was
slated to be sold to the Red Jacks mercenary company, but after the
leadership of the White Cloaks in the Saints Battle, Luminous wanted to
reward them.  Presently, Loedegan Close is acts as a second base of
operations for the White Cloaks, and there seems to be much activity
there much of the time.

I would add that I feel as though the gift of the Close was something of
a closure for the Whitegates.  I think Luminous was happy to be free of
this relic from a previous era; that in which his family was growing and
prospering.  With there being little hope that his name would live
another generation, pride gave way to necessity. 

I would also add that the gift of the Close was something of an injury
to the Red Jacks.  There was already no small amount of bad blood
between the Red Jacks and the White Cloaks (indeed, between the Red
Jacks and pretty much every professional soldier in the region) before
the gift, but afterwards, animosity and acrimony on the part of the Red
Jacks was all but enough to have them exiled from Dagger Dale.

The White Cloaks continue to steward White Cloak Tower up at the Castle.
  

## Liberty Hill Farm

The story of Liberty Hill Farm after Kythorn 1, 1480 is one of
prosperity mixed with sadness.  It also marks a new phase in the
openness of the Church in the dale, and a new phase of demographic
transition.

After Banitt gave his moving speech after the Battle, and was invested
by Bishop Whitegate, a flood of immigration came almost immediately to
the farm.  On the day he took the mantle of Magistrate, Banitt presided
over a village of 225 souls, most of whom were orphans.  As I write this
now, the population of Liberty Hill Farm is nearly 2000!  Banitt, long
known as the 'Pilgrim', declared that he was directed by Amaunator to
come to come to Daggerdale, and to fight her enemies.  He was given the
Blade of the White Saint to battle the Dark Garden, and he was meant to
stay in the ruined village of Liberty Hill to bring a new light to the
darkness that had taken it.  His version of this story is much more
eloquent and compelling. 

There was a tremendous amount of work to do to clean up the village,
much less start rebuilding.  But before a tenday passed after the
battle, the pilgrims started showing up; first, migrants from nearly
every farm in the dale arrived.  Later, farmers and workers from the
surrounding dales came.  And before the snows came, and in the spring
and summer after they left, pilgrims from distant lands arrived.  They
all came on pilgrimage to see the Blade, and to get a blessing from the
Pilgrim Magistrate of Liberty Hill.  Many stayed.  I would guess that
several thousand pilgrims have come through Liberty Hill since that
time, and as I have mentioned, enough have stayed on that the place is
the third-most populated area in the region, behind the towns of Dagger
Falls and Charity.

The cathedral at Liberty Hill is now very richly adorned; it has
received gifts and donations from every corner of Faerun.  However, the
centre piece to the place is the relic that Banitt is now the life-long
steward of; the Blade of the White Saint.  This sword simply lays on an
altar in the nave of the Cathedral, where anyone can simply walk up and
touch it, or even pick it up.  Indeed, Magistrate Harbaugh is often
there to thrust it into the hands of the pilgrims that have come to see
it.  He assures me that the blade is quite safe and secure, and I am
obliged to trust in him, though I can't for the life of me divine what
protections he has in place, beyond good will and the forbearance of
travellers.

The Tower of Liberty Hill, and the Table of the Sun that sits atop it,
were given to the village as a public property by Lumina Whitegate.  She
told me once that she could not bear to live in the place without her
brother, sister, cousins and all the children running around.  It is
just as well; the Tower has been turned into a public house, with a
significant number of rooms that can be rented by pilgrims, for whatever
they can pay (or not).  The upper level has been used to accommodate
Magistrate Invictus, and the dozens of Sun Blades that have been added
to the defence of the village.  These Sun Blades are from the new 10th
Company, "Shadow Sentinels", and are specially trained to deal with very
dangerous and cunning enemies.

So today, Liberty Hill Farm is a bustling and prosperous place.  The
fields are all under plow, the village is bursting at the seams with new
people and buildings, and the concourse-market, a type of open-air
affair that is generally frowned upon in the dale, looks like a part of
our own Waterdeep bazaar dropped down into the middle of this
wilderness; there are colours, sounds and smells from distant parts of
the continent that give this village a truly worldly feel.

The prosperity of Liberty Hill is marked with some sorrow, though, which
is felt by all.  This was the hearth of the Whitegate family; the
ancestral patriarch of the family founded this place, and his own
account (c.f. 'Life of the White Saint', Shefton Dale, 1477) of the
matter is moving.  Today, the only Whitegate living here (for 'here' is
where I write this very document) is my dear friend Lumina, and she has
stepped back from any sort of governance or privilege.  She owns a small
cottage at the corner of the village, and though she has a public life,
being an ambassador to new guests and pilgrims, her presence here is the
very symbol of power-waning. 

As sad as this fact of life is, it is just that; times change, and there
can be no doubt that the net change at Liberty Hill is profoundly good. 
It is so good in fact, that when I have concluded my duties in the
Order, I will return to Liberty Hill to spend the rest of my days there,
and finally to be buried in the yard on the west-facing hill of that
happy place.

## The Kingdom of Tethyamar

I would probably not be writing this as I am, if not for Tethyamar.  As
I have mentioned, and has been recorded in greater works, the Tethyamar
League won the day at the Siege of Lastsun, and effectively ended the
war, for now, with Netheril.  Netheril took such terrible losses that
they could not continue any sort of aggression.  Unfortunately, they
have never sent a delegation to discuss a truce, so we can only assume
that there is more hostility on the horizon, when they have the means to
carry it out. \
I will add presently that I find it strange that Prince Melagaunt does
not offer terms of any sort; the Netherese princes responsible for other
polities have taken a civilized, if devious, approach to politics. 
Melagaunt seems determined to wipe Daggerdale off the map, whatever the
cost.

At any rate, he will have a difficult time of it, now.  The East Gate of
Tethyamar was discovered in the Gorimdun Mine by the White Cloaks, and
this discovery brought life to the movement to unearth this ancient
dwarven city.  The dwarves showed up in the nick of time at Lastsun, and
though they suffered dearly for their ally-saving intervention, they
have continued to trickle into the dale ever since.  Monthly, more
dwarves are added to the population of the dale; at first, they settled
up at the Castle as preparations were made to descend into Gorimdun. 
Before the snows set in, several hundred dwarves descended with what
looked like a year's supplies.

All winter long, reports came back from Tethyamar.  They had indeed
found the eastern reaches of the Upper Halls of Tethyamar, and had
indeed secured them.  However, as one engineer explained it to me, it
was like they had just found and secured the first inn outside the gates
of Waterdeep; there was an impossibly larger city awaiting them. 
However, it was held against them.  The depths of Tethyamar had become a
thriving environment of deep-things and undead, and they would have to
fight chambre-by-chambre for any further gains.

In the dead of winter of 1481, the Tethyamar League officially changed
its name to the Emissaries of the Kingdom of Tethyamar.  They still hold
a tower in the Castle, and have likewise set up an embassy in the Town
as well.  This spring, they began construction of a grand fort attached
to the outer bailey of Tesh Valley Farm, and as I am told, there is a
field full of tents surrounding the place.  They are calling this small
fort 'Stationer's Hall', and it is to be used as a sort of supply-post
for the expedition, and also as a trading post with the dalesmen.  It
has rekindled the blighted economy of Tesh Valley Farm (see my earlier
report on this matter), and brought a good deal of excitement to the
region.

Presently, there is a dispute in the 'Kingdom'.  The heirs of the
Kingdom have been meticulously traced for the several hundred years of
their exile from the city; in fact, they have always made claims of
finding the place and resettling it.  This has been the stuff of dwarven
legend and fancy, until last year.  Now the claimants to the throne have
come forth.  As it turns out, the commonly acknowledged and rightful
heir to the throne was one Ghellin Thordbardson of Clan Iron House (Iron
House was the founding and ruling family for the entire duration of
dwarven occupation of the city.)  Ghellin died a decade ago, and left
behind twin sons.  These two, Tasster and Tezster are not on good terms;
with the discovery that they would indeed have a kingdom to rule or to
split, they have already done the latter. This has divided Tethyamar
before it has even been retaken.

I know that Gambledin has done everything in his power to reconcile the
brothers, as have many other dwarven political personalities, but the
two remain inextricably at odds, and a layer of complication has been
added to resettling of Tethyamar. 

## Peace and Prosperity in the Dale

After the Seige of Fort Lastsun was ended, and the forces of Netheril
were in full retreat, peace and joy came immediately to the dale.  By
then, the threat of the Dark Garden was completely eliminated; the roads
and farms were as safe as they had been for decades.  The war had left a
robust military apparatus in place, and when the direct threat from
Netheril ended, this apparatus was used to clean house, so-to-speak. 
The mountains around the Castle and the forests around the farms were
scoured mercilessly until the snows came that year.  Many of these small
campaigns forged heroes and stories to bolster the already-growing
collection from the War with Netheril, and the Saints' Battle.  By the
time the first snows came, which wasn't until Hammer of 1481, there
wasn't so much as a single band of frightened orcs left to trouble the
sleep of the happy farmers.

The dale had come through dark times, but their emergence from this time
was a glorious thing.  The faith of the people in the Old God and the
New God was feverish and well-rewarded.  The summer of 1480 lasted until
after the Autumnal Equinox, and the final harvests weren't made until
the end of Uktar. I can still taste the apples that came off the trees
when there should have been snow a foot deep; they were as sweet and
rich as any I've ever had, and are the perfect symbol for prosperity in
this previously war-torn region.

Of course, it is quite common for there to be a perceived level of
wealth and gain after a war; with so many dead, the resources left are
split between fewer and thus seem greater.  But I could point to
precisely-kept ledgers of the Gate Clerks that will show a marked
increase in all manner of trade that fall and the spring after.  This is
partially explained by the influx of migration to Liberty Hill and the
dwarves of Tethyamar, but there can be little doubt that Faith has
played a significant role here.  Despite the rift that the Dark Garden
tried to cause between the Old faith and the New, they were joined
together by even stronger bonds during that peace which followed.

The apple tree that was planted by Chauntea in the dale, was bathed in
the sunlight of Amaunator, and the ripest fruit imaginable was the
product.  This allegory has been illustrated in many recently-crafted
songs and poems (some by my friend, Troia Trustar), and in the many
weddings in the spring of 1481.  As I have watched the doings of the
young people of Liberty Hill this spring, there have been a dozen
weddings, and most have been inter-denominational.  It is an encouraging
sign, indeed. 

The plans of Netheril and the Dark Garden failed, and Daggerdale is
enjoying a level of prosperity unseen since Ardelusk's days.

## The Black Hand of Gaebril Tremalkin

Zenith Lord Whitegate made a decision about the Black Hand that
surprised all.  As I write this, the Black Hand is on display in the
Cathedral of Dagger Falls, in the southern transept.  It sits atop a
slab of the purest alabaster, and is not under glass, irons or locks. 
It sits so that any might go up and touch it, if they dare.  I will back
up just a bit, though.

At the conclusion of the Saints' Battle, and the investiture of
Magistrate Harbaugh, that same new magistrate pleaded to keep the relic
of the Black Saint beside that of the White, in the cathedral of Liberty
Hill.  Bishop Whitegate declared that the threat of someone causing
mischief with the Black Hand was something different from the threat of
someone causing mischief with the White Blade, should Banitt's 'security
measures' prove insufficient.  So, the Black Hand was taken away that
day.

Weeks later, the Black Hand was revealed in its new resting place in the
Cathedral in Dagger Falls.  The Zenith Lord agreed partially with
Banitt; it would be better to put it on display, and keep no secrets. 
He decided on keeping it in town rather than at the farm, siding with
his Bishop in this regard.

The Zenith Lord made this proclamation with the revealing of the Black
Hand;

                "Let any who would consider the path offered by Shar
come to the Cathedral of Dagger Falls, and there look upon the relic of
the one they would call 'Black Saint'.  Let them stand before the
vileness of this thing, and account for themselves the wages of Shar's
service; foulness, corruption of the flesh and the soul, and,
ultimately, death at the hands of the Just!  Let them, our enemies,
come; I will admit them unmolested into our own cathedral to shake hands
with their saint, and if they so choose to keep their dark path, they
will be released, again unmolested, until we find them again.  And if
they choose to turn away, we will welcome them into the fold then and
there!  And let us ourselves not shy from this relic; look long at it
every time you grace the cathedral, and consider the ruin that Shar has
inflicted on our home.  Look, and remember!"

## Companions

I should now disclose a lapse in my duties.  I wrote to Canoness Majarra
in early Kythorn, 1480, asking for an extension of my deployment in
Daggerdale, and it was granted in good faith.  I had much work to do,
wrapping up loose ends and looking at intelligence coming from the
front.  However, one of the main reasons that I asked for the extension
was to be with Magistrate Hoetmer, with whom it is now well known, I was
romantically involved.  Phin and I spent long hours pouring over the
vast amount of documentation that daily came to us in the Historic Wing
of the Yellow Abbey.  With the defeat of the Dark Garden, the lines of
communication were again reliable and prolific in their generation of
reports.

However, Phin's health started to fail the very night he woke up from
his Aetherial Sleep, watching over the reconnaissance of the Farm on
Mirtul 26.  It was the longest of duration and, he claimed, "most
profound" journey he'd ever made in the Aether.  To comment on what he
did, saw, and how it affected him in the Aether would be pure
speculation at this point.  Phin never told me, and he kept few notes. 
I do think he had a terrible time in his dreams, though.  His ability to
help others inside his journeys came at a cost, and I believe that the
effort he put into warning the White Cloaks about the trap that had been
laid for them was the final stroke.

As we worked daily on our intelligences, Phin became gradually more
exhausted.  On the 29th of Flamerule, just days before the trial of
Belladonna, and the conclusion of his travails, he went to sleep and
never woke up.  If he knew he was going to pass, he never made mention
or in any way prepared for it.  The very day that he passed, he spent an
hour looking over and editing some notes that he made about his theories
concerning his connection to Banitt, Iolus, and the others.  He never
did figure it out, as far as I know.  I am sure it is the last thing he
was thinking about as he passed.

I was not the last to see him, though.  In his final sleep in this
mortal realm, he again projected into the Aether, and had a long
conversation with Banitt and Iolus, visiting both men in a shared
dream.  Both of their accounts of this dream are identical.  Phin
gathered them on a mountain top that Iolus said was familiar to him.  He
said he was "glad to be at liberty, even if only in his sleep".  Iolus
became upset, mostly at himself at this point; He had sworn to get Phin
out of the Yellow Abbey, as he thought he was a prisoner there, of a
certain variety.  Phin did often complain about not being able to wander
outside the Abbey at liberty, further suggesting that Lumina and Iolus
were right in their assumptions that the old Magistrate was a prisoner. 
He just knew too much that the Church could not have leaving the
Historic Wing, much less the Yellow Abbey.

At any rate, Phin thanked the two men for their friendship and service
to the Dale, and commended them on their faith in Amaunator.  They
discussed what few unresolved issues there were from the Dark Garden
affair, and at length, Phin said to his companions "I see the Sun, it is
time to go."  I believe he passed at that very moment, and is now in the
Yellow Fields with his Lord.

After the trial and sentencing of Belladonna, I had occasion to travel
to Liberty Hill Farm.  I wished to see the villainess herself, and
wanted to oversee the preparations for her incarceration.  At this time,
the clean-up of the village was complete, and there was already much
rebuilding and many pilgrims and immigrants to the village.  It was an
encouraging sight, to say the least.  Most encouraging of all was my
welcoming; Troia and Lumina themselves were at the gates, wishing
welcome all those that entered.  I knew these women from a few meetings
before the war, and they immediately warmed to me, and drew me close.

I planned on staying at Liberty Hill for a tenday, to see things
through.  I ended up staying the winter, and after putting my affairs at
the Yellow Abbey in order in the spring, returned to spend my last 2
months in the dale.  I write this very report as I sit at the humble
kitchen-table of my friend, Lumina.

During the months of rebuilding and immigration to the village, Troia,
Lumina and I acted as a greeting committee.  We would see all new
visitors to lodgings and food, show them about, bring them to the
cathedral, take care of their children, and so on.  We met a dozen new
people every day, and helped make arrangements for hundreds of them to
move permanently to Liberty Hill.  All day, every day, we worked at this
task, though it was never a travail of any sort; the three of us were so
full of enthusiasm for this job, that we were thankful every new day for
the chance to do our part.

And every night, we three stayed together; many nights we would go to
the Tower, and entertain the newcomers, or just stroll at leisure.  Many
nights, though, Lumina was clouded in shadow, and we spent those long
nights fighting for her soul.  Every new day found her as bright as her
name, but I am sure that without our companionship, especially that of
Troia, who is a peerless counsellor, Lumina's soul would have been
lost.  Every morning, after her matins, she would mount the stairs of
the Tower, and would talk with Belladonna on her prison.  Many days she
returned from this duty with a shadow just behind her eyes, and these
nights, after our day's work was done, we would, as I have said, fight
for her soul.

As I write this, Lumina is mostly recovered; she has very few bad nights
now, and I think the routine of facing Belladonna every day, at exactly
the same time (12 minutes after sunrise), is now therapeutic for her,
despite the fact that Belladonna just stares back in silent hatred. 
Lumina has given up most of her worldly possessions, and only keeps a
small cottage, in which I shared a room with Troia this past year.  She
constantly refuses gifts from the people of the village, usually
redirecting them to the neediest families, few as they are.  I think our
battle for Lumina's soul is won; it has healed along with Liberty Hill. 
But there is, like Liberty Hill, one dark spot to be found in the place
that has been carefully caged and must be vigilantly watched over until
the end.

Of Troia, I will say that a lovelier woman
was never born.  She is beautiful from the inside, all the way through;

But Troia has one secret that she keeps, from most, if not from us.  She
spent the three nights of the full moon every month wandering the dark
forest around the fields of the farm.  She would be gone all night, and
come back just before dawn, to the amazement and puzzlement of all.  Her
nocturnal wanderings were well rumoured at, and known by all.  None
besides her closest friends, however, knew that she spent three nights a
month with her friend, Braxus.  Further, none save the sharpest rangers
even knew of the benign werewolf's sentry of the farm (or perhaps just
of Troia.)  She didn't openly discuss the matter, and it would take the
most insistent prising on the part of Lumina to make her even confess to
the matter.  Troia always insisted that her friendship with Braxus was
plutonic and I believe her.  Her walks have come to an end, though.  She
returned early on the first night of her walk in Ches, and wore a
complicated expression of grief and joy.

## Farewell to Daggerdale

As I write this, it is the 15th of Ches, 1481. 

I have concluded my affairs at the Yellow Abbey and given thanks and
regards to the clergy there.  I have offered them hospitality on the
Order if they find themselves in our Fair City, and they have likewise
insisted that I return as soon and often as I wish.  Bishop Whitegate
has been a generous host these last two years, and expressed sincere
regret that my time in the dales had concluded.  After I took care of my
(numerous) final correspondences, archiving, and farewells, I made for
Liberty Hill for one last tenday of the springtime Sun on the farm. 

I was glad to see Troia there; her duties with the White Cloaks have
kept her away since the weather became warmer, but as promised, she
returned for the Spring Equinox, and we have spent much of the day
wreathed in all manner of Chauntean decorations; I think I will be days
pulling leaves and such from my hair.  The air is so warm, moist and
rich that it is more like drinking an intoxicating wine than breathing. 
In fact, I've had a bit of both (shame on me, I know).  My companions
and I spent all day in the sun, watching young women chase young men in
Chauntean courtship games, while the adult citizens drink and sing, and
the smaller children race about, naked most of them, like little forest
sprites.  Truly a day of fresh beginnings, springtime every bliss-filled
moment.

It seems odd to be leaving on such an evening; when so much is just
beginning at the place I now call home, the place I must hasten from. 
But duty finds me packed and ready to leave again, and I will not record
my parting salutations to my friends and my home.  My pen will remain
forever hopeful of fond returns.

S. Estelmer
