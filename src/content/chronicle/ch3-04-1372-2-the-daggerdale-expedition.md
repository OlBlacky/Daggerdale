## Departure

The most reliable part of the story as I understand it starts at the end
of Mirtul, 1372, as the Daggerdale Expedition (DE) approaches Loudwater,
nearing the mountains that separate the Western Heartlands from what was
the vast desert, Anauroch. Much happened between the gates of Waterdeep
and the long inland-journey they made, but that part of the story I am
not familiar with.

Though others would join later on, the DE at this point was composed of
the following members;

- Boregar the Wanderer -- a ranger or fighter of great ability

- Maturin Horgath -- Paladin of Ilmater from the distant East *(GFD
  Note; 'St. Maturin' in the 15th C.)*

- Mourn "Raven"Raventree -- Noble warrior of Waterdeep

- Sparrow -- Arcane assassin? *(GFD Note: Ultimately ends the mage
  Yarkul)*

- Corrina -- *(GFD Note: Not the same as the Dalael bride, but we'll
  hear of her later)*

- Dalak Quickaxes -- Dwarven battlerage, now Proprietor of the public
  house in town

Loudwater was the last civilized place on the East Road at this point in
the journey, and the DE paused in this solemn town to re-supply. All
stayed in Loudwater for a time, except Boregar who argued that there was
no time to waste. And so, he lived up to his name and left early, to
wander the East Road.

It is important to note that the DE had already suffered loss; Mourn
Raventree was killed by assassins on the road before Loudwater, but was
thankfully replaced by the famous swordsman, Dorian of Sembia. Why
Dorian fell in with the DE is lost to history, but the impact he had on
our community is not.

The rest of the DE left a couple days afterward, and upon their travel
along the East Road, they received magical word from Beljuril that the
Neverwinter Company had been victorious. The grim news about the death
of the Harper Initiate Rufus is taken hard by Boregar, who was starting
to suspect some greater plot, and fears that someone has infiltrated his
order, or sold them out.

The DE comes to Dekanter and finds that the town is under quite a bit of
construction, and that they make no efforts to hide their allegiance;
banners of the Zhentarim and of Cyric fly freely, and there is clearly
much slavery here. The DE was loathe to leave this abomination behind,
but a great task had been set before them, and so they pressed on, the
desert just a day away.

Heading into Anauroch, the DE is disheartened to find that they have
traveled less than twenty miles after about four days, and soon after
that they learn that they have traveled in a circle for a long time.
Sand storms called 'Azirla Dahab' plague their passage, and it seems as
if though the desert was not going to let them pass.

## Meeting Grandfather

On the 16th of Kythorn, the DE, and Maturin especially, had an encounter
with fate. It was on this day that they encountered Grandfather, a great
werelion, and his consorts on a rocky heel jutting out of the hot golden
sands. Grandfather demanded tribute in flesh for passage, but none was
offered. Grandfather and his consorts attacked, and in a long battle
with the creatures, Maturin, Boregar, Dorian and Sparrow were all
wounded by the great werelion. Maturin, especially, took wounds numerous
and grievous enough that there was little doubt that he had been
infected. Dorian was spared his death by Tymora alone, as Grandfather's
great maw found his neck, and flung his limp body into the sands before
ripping at the guts of the dangerous battlerager, effectively ending his
career. In the end, they managed to repel the creatures, but their
wounds were severe. Here are the recorded thoughts of Maturin the Grey
from the next day;

## Infection

The next night, the DE arrived at the first Zhent oasis-outpost,
Vaerthyl. Before they would leave this outpost, Dorian, Sparrow, Maturin
and Boregar turn into were-lions and ripped the place and everyone in it
apart. The battle-rager also helped in the destruction, but in the end,
he was met by Maturin in his lion-man form, who ripped Dalak apart,
nearly killing him.

The next day dawned with little hope for the heroes. The whirlwind of
bloodshed, violence and destruction was but a foggy memory. The four new
lycanthropes found themselves each waking up alone and naked in an
unfamiliar part of the desert. Most managed to find their way back to
the oasis, and there they found the horrific scene of slaughter. Men
were ripped to shreds and many had clearly been feasted upon. This
caused the four infected members of the party to become sick to their
stomachs. Zhents and slaves alike had all been slaughtered. Horses had
been butchered, walls clawed through. The entire place was in absolute
ruin. Corrina had gone missing, and never again was she heard from. Her
story ends in obscurity here.

**Now they were five.**

A great pyre was made to burn all the corpses, and great piles of
weapons and such were made. Dalak was found, and tended to by Maturin
all day, but things did not look good for the battle-rager. Though the
remainder of the company looked for their missing comrade, at length
they all solemnly admitted that it was time to move on. The road
continued, and so did their quest. They left behind a companion that
day, and many of the items they treasured and needed for the journey.
But Maturin left behind everything. Ilmater turned His back on Maturin
that day, and the Paladin began his long lonesome trial.

The remaining four members would have perished if not for a fortuitous
meeting with a benign tribe of Neshaba.

These desert nomads were quick to help the DE, despite what all had
heard about the people of this land. It was not until much later that it
became clear why. This particular band bore the symbol of the Green Eye
of Muir'Quaggoth, and they must have known about the infection in the
veins of the DE.

After days of wandering the road, they came to the second oasis-outpost,
Olomaa. They decided not to tackle this one head on, but in their
attempt to circumvent the place, they were intercepted by the 14th
company of Fist mercenaries. They managed to destroy this company nearly
to the man, but they learned that these twenty men were of elite
training, and quick thinking.

## The Wanderer and the Quests

Days later, as they trekked through the sands, the DE encountered
another 'golden death' storm. This was the worst one they would endure,
and it was bad enough to sunder the company. Dorian was blown away
hopelessly in the storm, and the DE lost almost all the valuable gear
they had managed to retain after the slaughter in Vaerthyl. When the
storm finally subsided, Dorian and Sparrow were long gone, and no tracks
or trails were there to be followed. The desert had seemingly just
swallowed the men up mercilessly.

Three days later, and two nights before the full moon would rise again,
a boon came from the desert, and hope was put into the hearts of all,
but especially in the heart of the Forsaken Paladin. A wanderer came
from the desert. A man who seemed a beggar came over the dunes, and he
helped along the weakened Dorian, who he had found the day before.
Dorian had been staring at the sun and hallucinating, drawing his final
breaths. The wanderer followed the birds of carrion and rescued him.

This man, Shildevan the Wanderer, was a holy man of Ilmater. He was on a
holy pilgrimage to harsh lands everywhere, and the hand of Ilmater
guided him to the DE. He managed to bring all but Sparrow back together
again.

**Now they were four.**

Shildevan had favour enough with Ilmater that he could cure the horrible
lycanthropic sickness in the veins of the infected men, but all had to
agree to a quest to receive this blessing.

Dorian was to eliminate the threat posed by the Beholders, and that he
could not accept any pay for this mission. Though the clever Sembian
agreed to not accept a single 'copper piece' for the entire quest, he
did not realize the scope of his challenge. Indeed, his was the only
quest that would bring him back to the streets of Waterdeep over a year
later, to equally dire struggles.

Boregar was commanded to be true to his friends, but to return and set
right all the wrong he had left behind. This seemed like a great task
given all the evil they had turned their cheeks on during the journey so
far.

Maturin was commanded to free the slaves of Dagger Dale, and then to
return to the desert to slay the Chosen of Muir'Quaggoth, Grandfather.

So it was that these three men were cured of their infection, and were
set upon the greatest quests of their lives.

## Delivery into the Dales

Back on the road, the DE began to make solid headway after their
fortuitous encounter with the holy wanderer. They came to Addas Babar,
and secretly traded with some slaves for food and water. A small fight
ensued, and Boregar was taken down by the mage Farim, the only survivor
of the Vaerthyl massacre. Though he was later rescued by his companions,
the Gond Pistol artefact that Boregar had wielded was not recovered.

Days later the DE once again encounter the Nashaba, and this time their
headman Kabina assigns a guide to them, so that they may go through the
desert quicker. After many more days of travel and many more pounds had
come off the frames of the travelers, the Desert's Mouth Mountains came
into view in the East, and the hearts of the travelers leapt.

It should be said that all the obstacles, challenges, misery and atrophy
of the desert have not been detailed in this brief summary. The journey
was one of constant loss and doubt, and in recorded history it was not
known that four lonesome travelers could make such a remarkable journey
and live to tell about it, until these four came out alive.

While traveling in the bad lands between the desert and the mountains,
the four encounter the ogre-mage Yin Xu. They find they are unable to
hurt the creature before he is able to escape using his gaseous form,
but he is also outmatched.

After crossing the mountains, the four head down into the fertile valley
that is the border region between Dagger Dale and Shadow Dale. The
hearts of these men were renewed, though their bodies were just
beginning to recover from their journey. They traveled off the roads, as
they were weakened and had insufficient tools for making war, and knew
that the Fist would be close by.

They encountered a group of Zhent scouts as they neared Shadow Dale, but
they were rescued by a group of elves of the Region, who Boregar was
familiar with. The elves then escorted the DE to Shadow Dale, where they
arrived on the 4th of Eleint.

Even the Boregar was noticeably thin when the four turned up at the Old
Skull Inn. The others had the look of skeletons, and all were haggard,
filthy, and wretched. After they were cleaned up, and donned some new
clothing, they weren't long fitting in at the taproom. Crowds flocked
around them (despite their want for some peace) and asked many questions
of the men who had come from the desert to liberate the slaves of Dagger
Dale.

One of the guests at the inn that night was the noble fop, William
Cormaeril. This man had come from Cormyr, and claimed that he was the
rightful heir to rule Dagger Dale with the death of Randal Mourn. He had
a small retinue that included a half dozen Purple Dragons, and he
boasted that he would liberate Dagger Dale from the treacherous Zhents,
but he was not taken seriously by anyone who heard him.

Also traveling with him is his wife, Medea Rowan, and their two sons.

That night peace would not come easily. The half-elf assassin Dragon
entered the inn and made an attempt on Dorian's life, but was not
successful. She managed to escape before any harm could befall her.
Though she scorched and skewered Dorian, her most grievous wound was
upon his heart. The fool was beginning to fall for her.

## First Battle of Ananathar's Dell -- Eleint 5

The next morning, the epic scale of the events in the Dales was made
greater yet again. The order of the Broken Blade, including 8 knights of
Tempus and their retinue, rode into Shadow Dale with full pomp and
ceremony. They, too, announce that they will be heading to Dagger Dale
to put an end to the villainy there, and that they will not wait a day.
Their leader Sir Durnbold implores Cormaeril to ride with them and take
his rightful seat after a valiant battle, but his cowardice was great,
and the Broken Blade rode out that very morning without him.

As the Paladins of Tempus went west out of town, all took it as a good
sign. But then, the heavens gave ill portent. A green and black vortex
was seen in the sky, and it inspires panic in the village. As the sun
was coming up that morning and the vortex was swirling, the Church of
Lathander began its bifurcation. A fragment of the church lead by Rewel
Thunderstorm, the second-in-command of the church in Shadow Dale walks
out on High Dawnlord Cassimar's sermon. Thunderstorm insists that the
vortex is a sign, and that they should have sent all available
Lathanderites with Sir Durnbold to help in their noble quest.

By a reckoning put together by an Oghman in Waterdeep much later, *(GFD
Note: Kennyth Thames)* this was the morning that the slaves in the
Dagger Hills Mine uncovered the beholder tablets, and the vortex was
likely the manifestation of some god associated with the beholders.

That night, good news and bad both come to the DE in the inn. Sparrow
has emerged from the desert! He explains that he was also helped by the
nomads of the desert, and that magic sustained him. He brings ill news
though. That day he passed by the site of an ambush, and reports that
the Order of the Broken Blade have all been slain in ambush less than 20
miles from Shadow Dale. As this news spreads, so does the bifurcation of
the local church of Lathander.

The next morning, all surviving DE members (now they are five again!)
are together and recuperated. They plan to re-supply today, and to begin
their toil. That morning, though, more trumpets are heard, and Shadow
Dale once again seems to be the stage for a play of the Gods. The holy
trinity of Tyr, Torm and Ilmater march their brightest lances and blades
into Shadow Dale that morning. The following orders were present;

- Order of the Golden Lion (Torm), led by Sir Gethard Soulbright

- Order of the Lambent Rose (Ilmater), led by Sir Lexinauth and Father
  Eisley of St.Collin's Abbey. Both of these men refused to speak with
  Maturin.

- Knights of Justice (Tyr), led by Sir Wildermot Silverlance

One of the men in the tent-train of the OLR was Revered Brother Turin,
who was an old friend of Maturin's. The two had served in the army
together in Damara, fighting the Lich King of Vaasa. Turin was glad to
see his old friend, despite what politics where at work.

Word that day comes to Boregar from the local wood elves that Yarkul was
spotted at the Dagger Hills mine the day before, perhaps accompanied by
a beholder. They took something large and heavy from the mine and
marched away. Sparrow insists that the trail least cold lies in that
direction, but there were many questions about which task to start
first. There were 3 slave mines to free, and the town itself to
liberate. The choices were many, and there were many political
considerations now as well, with knights of three orders in town.

## The Second Battle at Anathar's Dell -- Eleint 7

The night of Eleint the 6th, a great conference was held in the Twisted
Tower. Lord Amcathra hosted a meeting with the DE, the knights of the
order, and the two leaders of the bifurcated church of Lathander. There
were many discussions as to what was the best course of action. As well,
Sir Soulbright sued for some troops from Amcathra, but he could spare
none. The only Shadow Dalesmen that would accompany the war party would
be a select few priests of Lathander from the Thunderstorm faction of
the church of Lathander.

Early on the morning of the 7th, a war host rode out of Shadowdale the
likes of which had not been seen in a score of years. The three orders,
accompanied by the DE set out. They had a retinue of priests of Ilmater
and Lathander in tow, as well as a few Shadowdale men who decided to
follow with what arms they could muster. Cassamar and Amcathra met them
on the west edge of town, wished them luck and apologized that they
could offer no further aid.

Twenty miles and most of the day later, the war host came to Anathar's
Dell. There, they found the small walled farm village burnt to the
ground, and the bodies of the slain Knights of the Broken Blade strewn
about, defiled and rotting. They were put upon a pyre and given rites by
the many holy men there. Their impetuousness was also cursed at that
time. The Broken Blade had marched with the Trinity for many days, but
would not abide the pace of the Ilmater tent-train, and thus found their
enemies two days too soon.

While the smoke billowed into the sky, an enemy host gathered on either
side of the dell. To the north, the Knights of the Black Gauntlet
gathered. This is the militant order of the church of Bane, and had just
recently been assembled after the resurrection of Bane. To the south,
the more formidable Brothers of the Black Fist, led by Baron Drake
assembled. This militant arm of the church of Iyachtu Xvim had been
growing for years at this point, and their main headquarters and
training ground was located in Dagger Falls at Brotherhood tower.

A brutal battle was joined, as the forces of good clashed with the
forces of evil. Paladins of many gods struggled against each other, and
in the end it is difficult to say who won or lost. The Knights of the
Black Gauntlet were utterly destroyed, but the Brothers of the Black
Fist were merely repelled. Baron Drake slew several paladins of all
three orders himself however, before he thought it prudent to lead his
forces from the field. In the end, the DE and the surviving members of
the war host of the Trinity held the field, but at great cost. And Baron
Drake was far from defeated. He laughed as his demon-steed bore him from
the field.

The battle rager Dalak was nearly killed that day as well. The Baron
delivered a blow that re-eviscerated the battle rager, and though it did
not kill him, it put an end to his fighting days. He spent the rest of
this story convalescing in Shadowdale.

Anathar's Dell is often referred to as Paladin's Grave these days, for
the 21 Paladins that were buried there.

*It is important to note that years later, a great cenotaph was erected
there. However, as the Spellplague hastened (and still hastens) the
growth of Cormanthor, the cenotaph and the knights were moved to my
grandfather's farm before I was born.-LW*

It is late into the night before the war host marches again, back to
Shadowdale, in acceptance of defeat and setback.

## Preparations for War -- Eleint 8-10

The next day was a day of many activities. A council is assembled again.
When the survivors of Anathar's Dell describe what happened to Lord
Amcathra, he voices his concern over the absence of the militant order
of the Church of Cyric, which is also strong in Dagger Falls. Cassamar
and Thunderstorm get into a heated argument over signs from Lathander,
and the right and wrong course of action, and Thunderstorm goes so far
as to say that Cyric was not at the battle because he was at work in the
heart of Cassamar, who should have sent troops to the battle.

The Boregar set out that day to get word from his allies in the woods
and surrounding country side. He visited the significant other in his
life, Silve Silverhand. This Harper and daughter of the famed Storm
Silverhand was a long-time lover of Boregar. The two had a lasting but
intermittent relationship. The two lived harsh lives that kept them both
on the road and apart, doing deeds they received little thanks for. This
day, they met as lovers and as colleagues. Silve had long ago planted a
man in Dagger Falls and had been receiving correspondence from him for

## The Tyrant's Mine -- Eleint 10-12

Boregar took the note and delved into the woods to meet with his elven
connection. He is given a magical elven cloak as a gift, and learns
about Yin Xu, the ogre mage that they fought in the desert. It seems
that Yin Xu is now stationed on the border region between the two dales,
but Boregar suspects that Yin Xu has been tasked with getting rid of the
DE.

On this day, however, William Cormaeril sets out on his expedition. He
boldly claims that he will have Dagger Falls liberated in but a few
days. The council is re-ordered, and Amcathra expresses his frustration
with the events unfolding here. He implores the two factions of the
church of Lathander to reunite, but they will not. He is frustrated that
each side will not agree to march together upon Daggerfalls and that if
the forces of good continue to attack in bits and pieces, then that is
how they shall end up. The council then decides that more reasonable
goals have to be pursued. Thus, they set the 3rd mine as their first
objective. Sparrow had a visionary dream on the night of the vortex that
he reveals at this time. He believes that Mystra was guiding him to the
3rd mine to pick up the trail least cold.

The DE sets out for the 3rd mine (in the Dagger Hills, where the tablets
were found). Today we know this as the Tyrant's Mine, but I'm not sure
when that started, or by whom.

On Eleint 11, they arrive at the mine and find it pretty much abandoned.
Doing some exploration, they find a pile of bones in a pit, but the
night is getting long. That night, Maturin receives a visitation from
'Torm' in his dreams. A great pillar of skulls is building into the sky,
and the death will only stop if he delves into the pit to find his
answerers. 'Torm' tells Maturin to follow the Tablets at all costs. This
puts him at a crossroads however, as following the tablets would put him
further from his quest and would leave Dagger Dale in the same condition
it is in now.

The DE spends the early morning the next day exploring the great pit,
and finds beneath the bones a pair of great doors carved with beholder
writing. Sparrow uses divinations and sees visions of 20 men (probably
followers of Cyric) lead into the pit, where they fight 4 beholder-like
monsters. He also has a vision of two huge tablets being taken from the
mine. Any further divination beyond the doors is impossible, and they
are completely impervious to magic, possibly made of Starmetal. They
find they are unable to open them in any manner, and quickly decide it
is time to free some slaves.

## Exodus from Chillholm -- Eleint 13-21

A couple of nights later, the DE is close to the Chillholm mine. That
night, Sparrow sneaks into the barracks and cleanly assassinates the
mage running the operation. Sparrow overhears that the Fist will be
leaving in the morning, but that the Garrison Captain there is upset
about that, with all the orc sightings of late.

Early the next morning, 11th Company of Fist ride out of the mine, and
shortly after, the DE strikes. In the drawn-out battle, the Garrison
there is slain almost to the man, and 6 score of slaves are rescued.
That night, the exodus to Shadowdale begins.

The next day while the exodus marches overland to Shadowdale, Boregar
and Dorian make a guerilla attack against the First at Rain Hill. They
light off a powder keg in the barracks and destroy almost an entire
company of Fist before making their escape.

The exodus back to Shadowdale was not eventless. Maturin had his hands
full tending to the failing health of the liberated men, and despite his
best care, many could not survive the dangerous travel home.

On the night of the 19^th^, the 2^nd^, 3^rd^ and 4^th^ companies of Fist
caught up with the exodus at Liberty Hill (it wasn't called that yet, of
course), and though losses were great for both sides, the Fist did flee
the field of battle, and the liberation of those slaves was complete. Of
course, my grand father was one of those at the battle, and his account
of it can be found elsewhere.

## Exodus from Daggerfalls & Arcane Assassins -- Eleint 24-26

The DE leaves for Dagger Falls with this mage, named Grimspite that day.
Arriving in Daggerfalls the next day, Boregar is surprised to see the
place. It barely resembles the Daggerfalls he saw the year before. A
great wall has been constructed of solid stone all the way around, and a
beautiful, fortified bridge has been built across the Tesh. From the
bridge, many bodies hang, including the corpse of Randal Mourn, which
has a slight necromantic dweomer upon it.

Inside the new Eastern Bailey, there are hundreds of slaves gathered.
They look frightened and malnourished, but they are not being abused.
The DE decides to take the slaves out of town right away, rather than
confronting Yarkul as a group, and Maturin begins the exodus of these
people.

Sparrow stays behind in Dagger Falls, and that night he gets into a
spell duel with Yarkul after sneaking into the Tower, and kills him.

next night Sparrow returns to investigate the Tower to see how the power
vacuum is filling in, and is set upon by another wizard and an
assassin/Zhent spy named Venosar. He is magically bested by Thagdul (who
if any knew was present in the dale, had not revealed it), and his had
is cut off by Venosar.

Despite being at the mercy of Thagdul and his assassin, Sparrow finds
himself waking up in a medicine tent in Shadowdale. To this day, none
know how he went from falling into unconsciousness at the Tower, to
waking in the Shadowdale, but his sanctity with Mystra likely has
something to do with it (and his escape from Anauroch a month earlier,
as you will recall). He had a dream vision from Mystra that he is to
establish a church in Daggerfalls, and in the dream, he still has his
hand. Waking up, he finds that his hand is gone, but that a spell-fire
blue spectre of it appears when he works the Weave.

It takes two days to get the freed slaves back to Shadowdale. They are

It cannot be overlooked that Gaebril Tremalkin appears in the story at

knightly orders fought and perished, and the differences in the zeniths

around this time that Gaebril began preaching his obscene faith to the
masses in the tents, and his transformation in history into the Black
Saint began.

## St. Maturin's Letter to Dalak Quickaxes, Eleint 29

*Dear Dalak,*

*It is nearly over, my friend. I trust that your health has improved
under Brother Turan's conscientious care and Timfast's cookery. I don't
need to tell you... on second thought; I'll remind you that the wounds
you suffered at Freedom Hill were grievous indeed. Your life is still in
the balance. This time next year we may be able to share an ale
together, but only if you follow Brother Turan's medications and keep
your head. I intend to see you hale and healthy when my work is done.*

*I wish to tell you that Baron Drake, the daemon we fought at Anathar's
Dell, is dead, and the Church of Iyachtu Xvim in Dagger Falls is broken.
If I get ahead of myself, it is to spare you the suspense, which is not
beneficial to you in your condition. I will tell all in turn.*

*Following our return to Dagger Falls with the refugees from Yarkul's
regime, one of the refugees told Lord Mourngrym about the destruction of
the Church of Bane in Dagger Falls by the forces of William Cormaeril.*

*At the same council meeting where the refugee's story was heard, we
learned that the Zhents in Dagger Falls had come into possession of a
primer for Harper Code and a list of the names and descriptions of known
Harpers. The Harper agents Garrick Shining and the druid Aerin had set
out only a week after us, coming southeast through the Goblin Marches
rather than through Anauroch with their news. They also brought the
unhappy news that only a handful of Harper agents remained in the
Western Realms.*

*Boregar, too, brought news. A Harper agent in Dagger Falls indicated to
us through a secret missive that the city was still rife with corruption
and fear. While Cormaeril pressed negligently on with reforms and grand
plans to ally the new Dagger Falls with Cormyr and Yûlash (Thagdul's new
stomping round), daemons walked among the citizens, and the murderous
cultists of the Godson brazenly flouted the new laws, kidnapping unwary
citizens and keeping them as slaves.*

*With the mysterious slabs making their way ever deeper into the desert,
Sparrow sought the advice of the Lady Mystra, and divined that we would
have no hope of catching them before they reached Addis Babar, the
largest of the Zhents' Anauroch outposts. Dorian and I disagreed on the
best course of action, but with the council of Boregar, a man named
Gaebril Tremalkin -- a holy warrior of a strange faith -- and Garrick
Shining, we decided to cleanse Dagger Falls once and for all.*

*Following the council, we were to get some rest before heading north. I
had barely caught my first wink of sleep in 48 hours when Dorian burst
into my tent to tell me that Sparrow was both in grave danger, and was
missing. I know not how the warning came, but Sparrow was nowhere to be
found. A hurried search was organized, and with a presence of mind that
still surprises me, I tracked Sparrow to the Old Skull Inn, where
patrolling guards had seen him head in the company of a beautiful
woman.*

*En masse, our party charged into the inn and up the stairwell only to
find ourselves confronting an obviously ensorcelled member of
Shadowdale's emergency militia. Our friend Dorian, hardly the most
level-headed of men these days, failed immediately and spectacularly in
his attempts at negotiation and began to scuffle with the man. I ran
downstairs with Boregar to enlist the aid of any spellcasters who
considered themselves decent enough to come to our aid and I am
surprised and sorry to say that only one soul in the bar saw his duty
clearly. By the time we had reclimbed the stairs, Gaebril and Dorian had
forced their way through the door that Sparrow and the woman were
sequestered behind. I came to the doorway just in time to see her figure
sprout wings, let out an inhuman screech, and launch upwards, smashing
through the skylight.*

*While Garrick fired an arrow up at the quickly diminishing beast, I
bade the spellcaster dispel whatever magicks may have lain upon Sparrow.
But even as his eyes cleared, I could see that his movements were
growing sluggish. I suspected that the foul thing had poisoned him in
some way. When I asked him, he indicated that it was so. I quickly
jammed my index finger down his throat to induce vomiting, and,
satisfied that he was safe, helped him down to the bar. By now, the inn
had emptied to watch whatever succubus or harpy had been preying on our
friend fly quickly north, towards Dagger Falls.*

*Our course was clearer than ever.*

*At noon, we set out for Dagger Falls, arriving the following morning.
Sparrow was afraid that he would be recognized at the gate, but whatever
forces had issued the capital interdiction against his return had
evidently not passed the order on. (Happily, Sparrow's spellcasting
ability has not diminished with the loss of his hand -- whenever he
attempts to cast a spell, a phantasmal hand of blue light appears to
work the somatics -- truly a sign of his sanctity, at least in Mystra's
eyes.) Passing through the gate relatively unmolested, we journeyed to a
pub outside the north gate where we were to meet a contact of Boregar's.
Why we must always seek out the houses of sin, I know not. Would it be
too much to ask to meet a contact in a house of worship once in a while?
I must admit that certain among us were not very circumspect about our
inquiries, which may have had the dire consequences that I will write of
soon.*

*Investigating the town, we confirmed that though Yarkul had fallen, the
evil grip of the Zhent churches still held sway. Boregar, as the
official ambassador of Shadowdale, sought an audience with Lord
Cormaeril. Though he could not command the sacrifice of his guardsmen,
and would not allow any volunteers, he indicated that, would we take up
arms with the Xvimites, his forces would not intervene. I will tell you
that I did not like this petty little man. Not only did he force his own
cowardice on his men, who could not have refused duty's clarion call to
rid the Dales of the Godson's evil taint, but he played us against the
Xvimites without committing himself to either hand, seeking, no doubt,
to ingratiate himself with whichever force proved victorious. A
Cormyrean by birth, he seems every inch the bastard son of a Sembian and
a Zhent.*

*With Cormaeril's pusillanimous blessing, we waited while Sparrow did
reconnaissance on the Xvmiite temple. It was locked solid, without
windows, having only metal doors, and being completely encapsulated with
both stone and metal, it seemed impregnable. Before long, Xvimite
priests appeared as from nowhere and began to work their accursed spells
against us. We had clearly been expected, and enemies came from every
street and alley to face us on all sides. Immediately, Dorian and
Boregar charged, while Garrick, Gaebril and I put our backs to one
another and began to face off against the companies of armsmen that came
at us from either direction. With his battery of spells, Sparrrow ran an
impressively judicious guerrilla campaign as support.*

*For over an hour we fought, with darkness, paralysis and the curse of
the Godson laying on us like a foul haze. But we would not tire, and our
courage did not flag. Just when we thought the infidels might break, a
gong sounded, and the remains of the force let out a hoarse cheer as the
terrible Baron Drake strode from the opening doors of the temple,
flanked by a coterie of experienced looking men.*

*Without hesitation, Sparrow threw a fireball into their midst.*

*Horribly burned, all but the Baron retreated into the dark fastness of
the temple, shutting the doors in front of them, with the Baron facing
us down. Though Garrick, Gaebril and I were still engaged with the
warriors, Dorian rose to the challenge of fighting Drake, his confident
swagger unchecked by the cambion's foul presence. My memories of the
battle are hazy, but I know that Garrick broke from our circle to attack
the Baron and was cut down, though whether by the Baron or a craven
armsman I know not. Sparrow barely managed to bandage his wounds in time
to save his life. Were he not with me now, I should have said he died.*

*Soon, Gaebril too broke from the fight and confronted the cambion with
Dorian. I could see nothing from where I stood, all being concealed by
the foul thing's darkness, but when the light returned, Gaebril had
fallen and Dorian danced with the daemon alone.*

*By now, we fought on the strength of our potions alone.*

*For what seemed an eternity, I was facing off against three trained
warriors, Boregar with another two and Dorian seemed at an impasse with
Drake. Then, in a critical instant, the Baron lunged at Dorian and
overcorrected. Dorian's returning blow nearly cleaved off the daemon's
arm. Seeing our chance, I broke from the circle and took a few short
steps towards the prostrate thing to bring down Raven's Tooth on its
black and battered head, cleaving the foul skull in twain. (Though I
meant to offense, Dorian has been quite curt with me since the
decapitation. I get the sense that I robbed him of 'the glory' of the
moment, in what would have been his finest hour. I cannot honestly
comprehend how someone's code of honour would see necessary bloodshed as
glory, and not as a hideous last resort. But then, I suppose you are
hardly a sympathetic audience for my point of view).*

*With a look of shock on its face, the daemon's body collapsed.
Immediately, its armour and weapon began to bubble and sublimate into a
foul mist. The remaining Xvimites broke, hard. But I do not think they
escaped. While we began to take stock of the situation, Boregar brought
Drake's head to Cormaeril, who seemed overawed. People began to float
back into the streets to marvel at what we had done, and the guard
quickly organized to finish the job and force open the gates of the
temple. At length it was done.*

*Never have I been involved in so fierce or lengthy a battle, and never
again do I want to see so much bloodshed. I have a new appreciation for
the stalwartness of your kind, my friend, and a new sadness in knowing
what sort of life you live. May Ilmater let my blessing upon you be
heard. We managed to rescue the men who had been enslaved in the bowels
of the temple, and through no small degree of argument with that bastard
Cormaeril, I was able to commandeer some supplies and care for their
upkeep, at Dagger Falls' expense.*

*Unfortunately, early in the afternoon, we discovered the fresh corpse
of Boregar's contact. Evidently, he had been murdered while the battle
raged. Dagger Falls is still without true peace, and I fear it will
never be.*

*As for our current situation, I will be as informative as I can. Dorian
has left us, and I do not know if he will return. His plans for the
future have varied erratically over the last week. I hope to see him
again, but will leave it to Tymora to see that our paths cross again.
Gaebril and I, and perhaps Boregar, are heading into the desert in
pursuit of the slabs, and I intend to hunt Grandfather to fulfill my
pledge to Ilmater. Garrick's plans are uncertain, though I expect he
will accompany us. As for Sparrow, he has decided to reconsecrate the
Xvimite cultists' den and build a temple to Mystra and Lathander in
partnership with Cormaeril. I pray that they are successful.*

*We have accomplished so much in the last months, and yet so much more
is before us. Of the four of us who crossed the desert together, I am
comforted that you, at least, have achieved peace, though at great cost.
I wish you and Balak all the best and bid you take care. Boregar and
Dorian have much to deal with, and I cannot escape the feeling that our
destinies are still linked with Dagger Falls and Anauroch. For myself,
doubt plagues me like a shadow, stronger now that the path seems clear.
I had another vision from Torm yesterve, urging me again into the
desert, away from Dagger Falls. There is yet to be a reckoning.*

*Ilmater guides us all,*

*Maturin.*

This correspondence to the retired battle-rager is probably the best
account of the final conflict between Drake and his Xvmites and the DE.

Dorian was frustrated with the lengthy decision making process, and rode
off to pursue the tablets without any help. Not far up the north road,
he encountered two Entropists who had just killed a Zhent wizard. Who
these Entropists were, and what their role was is lost to history, but
undoubtedly concerns the Starmetal in the area that is renders the Weave
inert. They convinced Dorian that his best path would be with his
comrades and his senses were returned.

It is worth mentioning at this time that Dorian had somehow been deeded
Castle Daggerdale by William Cormaeril, after Brotherhood Tower had been
thrown down. He considered this his new 'hereditary home', and rode hard
that night, and spent the night in the ruined castle. Dragon had been
following him the whole time, and the two shared their first night
together in those ruins. The next day, they departed. Dragon was
determined to return to the west by way of the south road, and Dorian
was going to return with this friends to Daggerfalls.

This history of the Castle is a matter for another time.

## The Temple of Cyric -- Eleint 30

Though a vision from 'Torm' had been given to Maturin, the DE could not
be convinced to leave Dagger Falls. They hotly debated the issue of
leaving, to pursue the stones, but many were unhappy with the situation
in Daggerfalls still. An assassin had killed Boregar's man, meaning that
there was still a treacherous puppet master somewhere in the council of
that politically-metamorphing town. Finally, it was decided that they
would stay and clean up this town for good, or ill, and allow the
tablets to escape into the desert.

A council was called in the Tower. All major players in the Dale were
present, and many things were discussed. The nature of the council was
changed to include Sparrow and Rewel Thunderstorm, and plans were made
to turn Brotherhood tower into a joint place of worship for Lathander
and Mystra. Further, Dorian, now Lord of Castle Daggerdale and a small
barony that surrounded it, had also landed himself title and a position
on the council.

Tempers eventually ran hot at the council, as Cormaeril would not give
in to any requests by the DE or other good parties at that council, and
finally Gaebril and Sparrow had seen enough. The two began a brief but
effective inquisition at that very council table, and an invisible man
was discovered behind Cormaeril. He was subsequently attacked and
chased, and through the remarkable, some would say miraculous, efforts
of the Harper Garrick, the spy was captured and interrogated. This man
turned out to be a Zhent spy named Venosar. Much was revealed by the
interrogation and examination of the journal of this rogue-mage spy. The
following details were uncovered;

- Yarkul was everyone's puppet

- Venosar had been a mediary between Thagdul and Cormaeril for months

- Thagdul was an ally, and Yarkul was his vassal

- Thagdul has been active in Yulash for at least 3 months.

- Cyric was influencing the Lathanderites directly by deceiving Dawnlord
  Cassamar

- Amaretta (the succubus) -- conjured by someone, Yarkul may have had
  some power over her, but her presence pre-dated Yarkul

- Venosar was 'our man on the council'. He was a feared assassin, sent
  to replace Yarkul. He charmed Cormaeril in the first place, and
  arranged the removal of the Banites and Xvimites

- Cyric does not appear to like the idea of the tablets going west.

The number of demons reported to be in Brotherhood Tower was told to all
the members of the council, in confidence, though each member was told a
different number. This is how Boregar's man was discovered and
subsequently executed.

Another important find in the bed chamber of Venosar was the fated
longsword of Morgan Lathkule. This blade had found it's way here, all
the way from Waterdeep, with Yarkul, who had kept it as a token of his
defeat over Raven. The blade was taken by Dorian, but ownership of this
legendary weapon did not end there; it would be wielded by Maturin
months later, but let me not spoil the surprise just yet. Allow me to
say only this; that blade, though delivered by evil hands, was sent by
the Good Fates, as you'll soon read.

With this information, the path the DE would take became clear to all
except Dorian. They would rid the town of the Cyricists when they were
healed.

All the healing resources of the Tower were mustered, and after the DE
had rested for about ten hours, they attacked the fortified church of
Cyric in Daggerfalls.

This was another battle of epic proportions. The DE, accompanied by the
now clear-thinking Cormaeril and his elite hearth guard, staged a
three-pronged attack upon the vile lair of these villains. During the
attack, it was learned that Morgul the Vigilant Talon (high priest or
Cyric in Daggerfalls) had been amassing an undead army. The ranks of
many species of undead bolstered the ranks of priests and warriors of
the Baneson servants, and the battle was difficult. In the end, though,
the Church of Cyric was also completely destroyed, and the Heroes were
praised with great praise.

## Departure - Harvestide

By the time things cooled off in Daggerfalls, Harvestide was upon the
Dales. Winter was coming, and the DE were overdue to leave. All signs
indicated that the Tablets were weeks ahead of the DE, but they planned
on catching them anyway.Daggerfalls had become a place of peace again in
those days. Rewel Thunderstorm, though having made peace with Cassamar,
still begat the church of Lathander in Daggerfalls. Sparrow stayed in
this town too, to do his goddess' bidding, and opened his temple in
conjunction with the new Morning Lord's Temple. The three evil faiths
were not heard from or seen in Daggerfalls for many years to come, and
peace was upon the place.

But Daggerfalls is doomed to have some turmoil. In the wake of the Black
Saint (though he wasn't called that yet), a cult of Shar blossomed in
this town. An oratory of Gaebril, given in the Eastern Bailey convinced
many to join this faith, to help them enjoy the new lives they had, and
to forget the evils of the past.

An oratory given by Maturin that very day also spawned an intense, but
briefly lived following of Ilmater. Until the height of the Spellplague
and the God Deaths, the faiths of Lathander and Shar were the most
influential in the Dale and in the town itself. The temple to Mystra was
also a very busy place, and it is well known that this attraction
brought many practitioners of magic, and their money, to the town every
year.

## The Road West

Though the first Harvestide celebrations in over 20 years were being
held in Daggerfalls, the DE stayed only until after breakfast of the 2nd
of Marpenoth. They took to the road, traveling as light and as fast as
they could. They intended to catch the Beholder Tablets, but hey would
not do so this winter.

Less than half a day out of Daggerfalls, the DE was ambushed by the 5th
Co. of Fist, Gumbottom's Guard. This company of ten hill giants was lead
by Yin Xu at this point, and his leadership nearly proved effective
enough to turn back the DE. Several were hurt badly, but they still
managed to defeat enough of the giants to route them. Clearly, the road
was still being watched by the forces of the Beholders, and the journey
would not be easy.

A few days after that, the DE was nearly exterminated. They encountered
beholder-kin upon the road. A large, tree-like beholder attempted to
have the DE turn back by using magical suggestions, but this proved
ineffective. A battle broke out, and other beholderkin joined the battle
to protect the overseer tree-beholder that tried to end the encounter
peacefully. The DE was overcome by these foes, and if not for the
intervention of the Entropists, all would have been lost.

These two men, now introduced as Amon and Zuth, of Chessenta, explain
that they were pursing the Tablets west, but realized they were not
suited to travel in the desert. They turned back, hoping to find a way
West through the dangerous lands to the south. The two men had a long
discussion with the DE. They explained that two beholders that the DE
knew of, Zantiid and Xulla were simply 'second rank' beholders. They
knew of the staves, but little about them. The two men seemed most
determined to get to the Tablets, but did not state their purpose
entirely.

## Addas Babar

The DE was soon in the desert again. Just as the badlands begin to give
way to sands and dunes, one finds the citadel of Addas Babar, perched
high upon an island of rock overlooking the first oasis on the long road
through the desert.

The DE decided they would not circumnavigate the outposts on this
return-journey. Boregar was on a quest to put to rights all those wrongs
he had left behind, and the DE would have been without him if they would
not fight his battle with him.

The DE made short work of this fortress, and left it a grave of Zhents
and a ruin.

## Bhaerlith

Bhaerlith was a difficult conquest, given the heavily fortified and
geographical nature of the place. At length, however, the DE were
victorious, and the fortress was liberated. Of the bounty found in this
fortress, the Gond Pistola was recovered and returned to Boregar. Many
other powerful tools of war were also recovered.

The many slaves that were there stayed on. Many were devout followers of
Ilmater, and though Maturin had clearly been excommunicated, they took
it as a sign that they too should isolate themselves and endure the
lonesome desert.

To this day, Bhaerlith is an abbey dedicated to Ilmater. The wandering
priest Shildevan came to the abbey not long after the DE had departed,
and assumed leadership of the flock.

## Olomaa

Travel through the desert was made somewhat easier this time around,
though Boregar worried that they would not make it out before winter. He
explained that even the native nomads of this land brave the underdark
during winter, rather than face the harshness of Anauroch's cold season.
They DE was making good time, though, as they were more effectively
supplied after each fortress they sacked. It was the plundered resources
of these forts, plus the hard-gained-wisdom from their first crossing
that made their return-crossing possible.

At Olomaa, they found that they did not get there in time to sack the
place. The fortress was in ruins, and not a soul could be found upon
initial arrival. Boregar investigates further, and finds that a lone
priest of Cyric is living there. This man, Ful'Zan, explains that a
beholder and the Fist came through and killed everyone. This settlement
had been the desert bastion of Cyric, but the temple is now in ruins,
and the sole survivor of the place has resorted, not with much loathe,
to eating the flesh of the dead flock of Cyricists there.

When Maturin shows up, Ful'Zan shrieks and hisses, and says "You didn't
leave when you were told! Now you can't catch it!" Apparently, this
crazed priest knew something of Maturin, and although the Boregar
attempted to subdue the man, and he makes off into the desert. His trail
could not be followed.

The DE stays on here for a day to burn bodies and desecrate the remains
of the Temple. They ponder the visions from 'Torm' that were visited
upon Maturin back in Daggerfalls, and conclude that Cyric was posing as
Torm again (the 'Fool Knight' being his favourite god to impersonate) to
motivate the DE to follow the Tablets, and leave the church of Cyric in
Daggerfalls alone.

That Maturin didn't follow the advice of Cyric in the guise of Torm is
to his credit, as well as the credit of his friends and advisors, who
saw their duty clearly. Cyric had been taken completely off the board
with the cleansing of Olomaa.

## Maturin and the Lion

Many weeks later, the DE arrives back at Vaerthyl. As Boregar had
feared, winter had come and the DE where trapped. They prepare the
ruined keep there for their occupation throughout the winter.

The winter is cruel in Anauroch, to say the least. The remaining five
men of the DE train hard all winter, but on New Year's Eve, the time of
reckoning for Maturin and Grandfather is nigh.

Late that night, as the full moon broke through the relentless clouds
and snows, and the eye of Muir'Quaggoth was seen by the Grey Knight in
its radiance. A great roar is heard off in the desert somewhere, and the
DE do what they can to equip Maturin for the fight. Not long after,
Grandfather lets out a roar just outside the doors. Maturin takes the
floor, braced for battle, while the other 4 wait at the stairs. They
know that this can be a fight between Grandfather and Maturin, alone.

Grandfather crashes open the door and stands on the threshold while
Maturin calls out the beast;

*Grandfather!*

*In the name of Ilmater, I call you out and command you to lie down
before me.*

*I command you to repent your wrongs and forsake your evil god.*

*I accuse you, scion of Muir-Quaggoth, of carnage immeasurable.*

*Your legacy of pain and suffering lies heavily upon Faerun.*

*I am here to cleanse it.*

*In the name of Ilmater;*

*In the name of those who have suffered;*

*In the name of all that is holy and good;*

*Repent and prepare to be destroyed.*

Grandfather growled low and strode into the room. The two god-fated
combatants stared at each other for a moment, and then the Lion sprung!
From the outset, things looked horrible for Maturin. He was knocked over
and pinned right away, the giant lycanthrope poised above his prone
form. The Lion bit deep into the knight, and tore and tore. All the
while, however, Maturin was pulling the beast onto silver-plated spikes
upon his breast plate, and making what attacks were allowed him with the
Lathkule blade; this blade having been forged and enchanted in Waterdeep
for the eradication of lycanthropes. The blade was thrust up through the
beast's belly, and it gave a great roar before delivering one final bite
to the knight. It tore deep into his neck, and then both combatants
ceased their struggle.

Maturin was hardly perceptible from beneath the giant beast, and neither
were moving. When his companions came to his side however, and hefted
the heavy monster off him, they found him still breathing. Though he was
covered in blood, both from himself and the Lion, they could make out
the shape of a gray tear under the Knights right eye. The knight
breathed, and his companions helped him to his knees. Maturin stayed
kneeling for what seemed like an eternity. His breathing caused him to
heave, and he steadied himself with the bloodied blade that had slain
the huge Lion that lay at his side.

He looked up, and the grey tear sparkled for just a moment, and Maturin
nodded wearily. At that moment, a scraping was heard at the
wind-assaulted door. Warburden had returned, and stepped in to nuzzle
his masters bloodied head. With what strength he had, the weary knight
raised one bloodied gauntlet to stroke the beasts great face in silence,
while his companions, silent as well, looked on.

Dorian was heard to whisper "Ilmater be praised".

This final portion of the Daggerdale Expedition is most eloquently told
through the words of the Grey Knight, found in his memoirs years after
these events;

*Ilmater\'s boon \'neath the green-eyed moon*

*Did tame the were-lion\'s wroth;*

*The desert trails had their secret tales*

*For the sons of Muir-Quagoth;*

*But a Damaran knight is an outlandish sight,*

*To rise from Anauroch\'s haze*

*And a night of fear in the Rogue Dragon Year*

*Was Grandfather\'s end of days.*

## The Story Ends and Continues 

Much more could be said of what happened to those heroes after the death
of Grandfather, and their return to Waterdeep. But with regards to the
role they played in Daggerdale, this chapter of the story is concluded.
In the spring of 1373 they continued on to Waterdeep, hunting beholders,
Zhents and Drow with their other companions whose company they parted in
Phaulkonmere two years earlier.

I wish I knew what happened to the tablets, to the wizard Thagdul, to
the other Zhents and the drow, but I do not. The world shortly after was
thrown into the chaos of the Spellplague, and only during my lifetime
have we seen a reconnection between the Dales and the Western Heartlands
(despite the efforts of Returned Netheril).

I do know that Dorian returned to the Castle, and I think he's still
there in some evil form or another.

I know also that the Black Saint returned in secret in the early 1400's;
his presence was whispered of enough to be more than a rumour, but he is
dead now, though ironically not forgotten.

Maturin too is dead. I am told that the remains of his body are at the
abbey at Bhaerlith, which remains free of Netherese influence despite
its proximity.

Unfortunately, Sparrrow Flame-Handed, who served as the high priest of
Mystra for decades even after her death, also vanished in the tumultuous
years of the Spellplague, though his body is not accounted for. He was
last seen with my father on the plains of Netheril, facing off against
abominable agents of that evil empire. Wielding what magic he could, he
held off a company of monsters while my father and his soldiers
retreated from an impossible battle. I in fact owe my life to Sparrow,
as this was years before my birth.

The Daggerdale Expedition and its allies was made up of other mortal men
whose names I cannot find in this age, though evidence of their passing
remains. Of the Harpers, Boregar, Garrick & Aerin, there is no further
mention I have found (and I have searched), but there are many young
boys named Boregar in this Dale and Shadowdale today, and Harper's Court
outside what was Brotherhood Tower, is named for them.

I will return to an accounting of some of these loose ends someday when
my duties are either lessened or shared. But for now, I remain,

*Luminous Whitegate,*

*Lord of Castle Radiance and High Commander of the Order of the Lambent
Shield*
