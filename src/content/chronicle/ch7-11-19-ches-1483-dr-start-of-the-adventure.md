## Opening Narrative -- 07h00 -- Governors' Hall, Chauntean Conservatory

*Governor's Hall is still largely incomplete. A year ago, it was 3
smaller structures that have been connected together and reinforced.
While the exterior is mostly done (there is still some scaffolding here
and there, and stacks of building materials that are being completely
ignored on this Vernal Feast day), the inside is less than ½ complete.
Though there is no work happening today, there are tools and supplies in
every room and corridor, and you can imagine that it must be quite
chaotic here when the tradesmen are here upgrading the hall.*

*The main entry room is probably 80% complete, and a fine desk of new
quarter-sawn oak sits atop a floor of Tethyamari (as they prefer now to
be called) flagstone, and a large fine carpet. The clerk behind the
desk, who only a few weeks ago you submitted your application for the
Contest to, Marlee Willow, happily escorts you to one of the back
corners of the large and maze-like building, past more renovation
obstacles, having to push past many other people coming and going.*

*You can hear the Chauntean Chambre before you get to it; there are
scores of people in a room a little too small for them, and quite a bit
of laughing, jeering and carrying on happening. Past a set of great 10'
high arched oaken doors is the Chambre. The room is a grand
conservatory, with the southern wall and half of the ceiling being made
of fine Charity leaded glass. Like other rooms, this one is also
unfinished, and you have to imagine what it will look like when it is
completed; it will be lush with all manner of flora, but today, there
are but a few trees planted, the rest of the planting beds awaiting
construction. The half of the room that isn't glass is all Tethyamari
stonework, new and fine.*

*A small makeshift stage has been assembled at the west end of the room,
whereupon a half dozen people are making casual conversation, while
surveying the room while you and a dozen others file into the
conservatory with the 40 or so that were there before you.*

*Aside from the people on the stage, who are clearly the members of the
Adjudicating Committee, the most obvious people in the Conservatory are
the 4 other factions in the competition besides yourselves.*

*You take a moment to take stock of them, and most aren't a surprise.*

*Bargle Battlemaster is there with his Free Defense League mercenaries.
There are a dozen of them, but their team of 5 who will be participating
in the competition stand out, being adorned with thick grey tabards that
have been presumptively embroidered with a purple thistle over the
heart.*

*Hastle Redwater is the loudest in the room, and is making crude jokes
surrounded by a dozen men including some of his best lieutenants, and
his team of 5, who wear red cloaks and sashes that are clearly newer and
cleaner than their more hardened and experienced fellow soldiers.*

*Captain Iolus Warburton has come down from the Castle to introduce his
new squad of White Cloaks. Warburton has a shimmering nimbus of light
around his head when you see him on the proper angle, a clear sign of
his sanctity. With him is the Administrator, Alen Reks, lean and
nervous, and dressed in the best clothes someone could have bought 20
years ago. The only other White Cloaks are the 5 new recruits who form
their team.*

*Finally, there is the other newcomer to the arena besides yourselves,
The Standing Stone League, headed by a merchant named Factor Belwintle,
who has recently procured a building in the Eastern Bailey which is now
known as the Standing Stone Factory. Belwintle is a round and sweaty man
in clothes just fine enough to not be offensive, and he is positively
beaming in the room today, surrounded by his small team of 5.*

*Aside from yourselves, there are also 2 squads of Sunblades in the room
doing their best to not stare at the different companies and the
impressive-looking men and women arrayed before all here.*

*The FDL mercenaries are quiet and smug for the most part, and they are
clearly sizing up all the competition in the room, while the Redjacks
make leering and jeering gestures to their competitors with little shame
or grace. The White Cloaks talk politely among themselves, and a small
group of Sunblades whom they seem to know, while Administrator Reks
nervously paces around the perimeter of his group doing his best to look
important.*

*Just as you have taken all this in, from somewhere deep within the
building a bell starts to chime. Silence washes over the room as people
start to turn to face the men and women on the stage, who are presently
straightening jackets and dresses and getting ready for an address.
Through the great windows, the earliest hints of the clear sunrise are
turning the sky from deep to lighter grey.*

*The well-dressed and well-groomed Ambassador Whitmore begins the
address by clearing his throat and politely nodding for consent from the
rest of his council flanking him.*

*"I would normally prepare a speech for an occasion like this, but I
have gotten to know quite a few of you over the last couple of years
quite well, and I know you don't like them, and that you are chomping at
the bit, as they say, to slog on and get stuck in." He pauses to survey
the room, and again looks to his colleagues to the left and right, and
is about to continue when Redwater interrupts him.*

*"Well say fucking something, or just let us go. I have a contest to
fucking win!"*

*A brief round of laughter mixed with sighs and a coupe of gasps play
out before Whitmore smiles, straightens his jacket for the third time
and continues.*

*"Yes, right. Well, that's the spirit, Redwater! I hope you're all as
buzzed as your gentle colleague! We have quite an agenda before us,
haven't we? Yes we have!*

*"You've all seen the various Measures we will be using to gauge your
suitability over the next tenday, so I won't recount them. You know all
the details we'll give you on three of four of the measures. What you're
really all here to learn is when you will be put to your martial tests.*

*"You are presently being provided with the maps and information that
were promised in your acceptance letter from the day before last."
Indeed, we he is speaking, Sunblades are now walking among the crowd,
handing to the captain of each team a neatly tied bundle of papers and a
small bag of coins. "On the top of that bundle of papers is a diary for
your Military Prowess challenges." And with this, all eyes now turn on
the captains who are quickly untying their bundle, and looking over
their schedule, while their teams anxiously await the news. From the
Redjacks, there is more laughing and jeering, this time clearly directed
at the Standing Stone League, who are doing their best to not look
cowed. You look at your own schedule;*

> *Ches 20, White Cloaks, 9 bells*
>
> *Ches 22, Redjacks, 2 bells of the afternoon*
>
> *Ches 24, Free Defense League, 9 bells*
>
> *Ches 28, Standing Stone League, 2 bells of the afternoon*

*Your hearts sink a little, as the reality of the competition strikes
you. You'll face the White Cloaks in the arena in 26 hours from now.
Looking around the room, you see that the schedule has settled the crowd
with the weight of its reality. Even the Redjacks are only slightly
braggadocios. Looking to the nimbus-limned Warburton, he salutes you
respectfully, while behind him Reks is gesticulating nervously.*

*"Ahhh...serious business is this not? We are all here because we are
looking to the future of Daggerdale, and indeed, the peace of the
region. But we'll have to spare those lofty words for nine days, won't
we? There's sweat and blood and tears ahead before we can look to that
eastern horizon, and I'll bid you all "good luck, and play fair" and
conclude this ceremony forthwith!"*
