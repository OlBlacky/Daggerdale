The pen is mightier than the sword! Ha, but not the
axe! Still, here I am, waging what I hope will be battle from beyond the
grave. For though my body will fail, hopefully in glorious battle with
axe in hand, these words may live on, and hopefully lend courage to
future generations of warriors.

But enough of my prattle. I must take these notes while they are fresh
in my head. For I am not a scribe by nature, and the battle to sit in
one place and record the day's events is the most difficult for me.
Tonight, Tempus has blessed me with no sleep, after days of great and
terrible news.

Though I write this on the 16<sup>th</sup> (or the wee hours of the 17<sup>th</sup> I
would guess), I must back up an order things in my mind.

On the 13<sup>th</sup>, with the camp at Greenwood secured the day before,
Chrysalis set out for The Tower, and secured a great victory there. I
will not recount it here, as their honour is not mine to share. However,
when news came of the death of Karn, there was a great roar of triumph
in the camp, and the command elements and senior staff of the camp were
overjoyed that this great victory had been delivered. For my part, I
gave thanks to Tempus for the bravery of the group, especially Ser
Rowan, who by the report I've seen, charged headlong into the darkness
that the Beast didn't think he'd dare go.

This victory has been dulled somewhat by the terrible discoveries in the
basement of the tower, which the Beast had laired in for nearly a year.
So much wickedness in such a short amount of time is nearly
incomprehensible to me.

The victory at The Tower took the entirety of the day, and started with
an early-morning battle in an evil glade 2 miles to the east of us,
along the old road. Here, the final chapter of the cruel story of
Garadin Gorehammer was written and closed, and I'll say no more.

It is also worth noting that Chrysalis saved a poor soul, Jendwendle of
Hillsfar, and repatriated the man to our camp. Jendwendle's mind has
been turned to mush after a winter's captivity with the depraved Beast
of the Tower, and he is presently being sent to Charnel Priory in
Charity, to see if he can't be knitted back together with time and the
Sisters' skill and faith.

By the afternoon of the 14<sup>th</sup>, Chrysalis arrived back in camp, and I
think the Captain was surprised to see how much work had been done in
just 24 hours. The men had cleared a substantial portion of the hill,
the sawmill was set up, and a comfortable camp had been struck among the
old ruins.

A council was struck immediately in the command tent, and preparations
made for the Sun Blades to garrison The Tower in force the next day.

##
Andi Autumnhill 

Late in the morning of the 14<sup>th</sup>, the halfling girl, Andi Autumnhill
(who's family operates the Inn in Gryphons Farm -- yes, I still use the
older name of this place), arrived unexpectedly in our camp, accompanied
by 100 chickens (mostly stout laying hens and a few very angry roosters)
and her two trusty hounds (a pair of clever Cormyrean Shepherds). I
mention the girl because her chickens became the target of the Fey-Thing
raids we've endured since. She's a brave and inspiring creature, and I
have instructed the men that her presence here is an opportunity to
learn about different types of bravery and battle, forbearance and
adaptation. For she made the trek here by herself, through a dangerous
wood, to camp with violent men and women in an uncertain frontier. We
could all do with a bit of the small-folk brand of courage.

Her chickens have attracted some unwanted attention, in the form of
Fey-Things that hunger for them. She has lost some small number of them
to tiny Fey-Things that stole into the camp. The men have been
unsympathetic, having had plans to eat the birds themselves (not
realizing the value of eggs for a year versus a roasted chicken today),
and it is a struggle of leadership for me to keep them from harassing
the girl and her fine birds.

## The Tower Garrisoned, Evils Discovered

Chrysalis left early in the morning of the 15<sup>th</sup> with the entire 7<sup>th</sup>
Co., train and all. Their intention was to garrison The Tower and hold
it as the next strategic staging point for our Grander Mission. Like a
cat grasping at wriggling prey, we will not loosen our grip until a
better grip presents.

Sgt. Birchgrove's Swift, Mareeseah, brings me regular correspondence,
and dutifully waits for me to give it something to send back. If I'm
honest, the bird makes me feel dim-witted. It sits looking at me while I
read Birchgrove's delicate hand and precise words, and awaits my penning
a response. I disappoint it regularly.

Though The Tower has been taken and the Garrison is hardening their
position, the basement of the place is an evil too much for some of the
men and especially, I should think, the women. It is full of corpses of
victims, some expected some not (where some of these poor souls came
from and how they ended up in this warren remains unclear).

Farmountain himself has been forced to lead the cleanup of the basement
levels, a task that is a constant battle with sanity. I will leave the
evils of The Beast of the Tower off these pages, save to mention that I
think some of Chrysalis are struggling with their encounter with the
Beast and his savagery. **Though The Beast is slain, his wickedness
cannot be undone, especially in the dreams of those that faced him in
the dark.**

Chrysalis stayed the night of the 15<sup>th</sup> at The Tower with the 7<sup>th</sup>,
and departed on the 16<sup>th</sup>, which would prove a very long and perilous
day.

## The Mysterious Temple

The fair morning of the 16<sup>th</sup> finds Chrysalis leaving The Tower to
return to Greenwood Camp, by way of scouting the lee of the hills to the
E-NE of our position. There, they were drawn into a curious ambush by
Yellow Tooth that I would later learn are the Gukk Warband from
Alavandor Farm. We have learned from perilous experience and from a map
discovered in The Tower that the forest is watched in many places by
some sort of witch-spy devices. I don't understand them completely, but
their tactical and strategic importance is clear and efficacious.
Chrysalis has been observed in most of it's travels in the wood, and the
goblins from the Alavandor Farm laid a trap for our Captain.

The Captain and her team, of course, fought through the trap and
defeated 'Swarm Boss Gukk' (as a report from Birchgrove I received late
tonight names him), but discovered that the trap had been laid inside a
strange temple. Lucan is quite bent over this building, and is wringing
his hands about not being able to identify it. He has sent messages out
for intelligence on the thing. I told him we could go and knock it down
and be done worrying about it, but I don't think he or the Captain
support my resolution.

##
The Blue Glade & The Fey Courts

Shortly after lunch we had the second of two Fey-Thing raids, this time
much worse. Ms. Autumnhill lost a few more of her hens to what turned
out to be things called Grimples and Pixies, but we have discovered a
much greater Fey problem. I'll skip ahead a bit here. The raid itself
was brief, and ultimately resulted in our Mason, Billy of Charity, being
lured away. His tools had been stolen by the Grimples, and it appears
that this may have been quite deliberate.

The creatures that took the tools led a false-path for us to follow, but
Matron Thickwickle was not fooled, and the true path of the Fey and
Billy Mason was soon taken up, which lead Chrysalis to The Blue Glade,
and their battle with Grimples, Sprites and a creature known as Vainya,
some sort of evil Pixie.

Though Mason was recovered, and many Grimples destroyed, Vainya escaped.
Chrysalis managed to befriend a half-dozen Pixies, who turn out to be
part of something called The Limestone Court. There is a
previously-unknown layer of politics in Cormanthor that we have just
discovered tonight, and it intersects directly with our Mission.

Though the Sprites seem to despise our Sembian for some reason, they are
overly fond of Master Hawkwinter, and gave him the following
intelligence;

**Contessa Calix of the Limestone Court**

She is young and relatively new to the area, though she was here 30
years ago, and was friends with some of the farmers at Greenwood.  She
is currently the prisoner of the wicked fey creature known
as Dol\'Rullian\
\
**Contessa Quercina of the Oaken Court**\
The Dryad that has lived in the area for more than 100 years, and was
bonded to the Great Oak Harngorn.  She has been in a great depression
since Harngorn was cut down in 1441.  Current whereabouts unknown.\
\
**Of Quercina, Masra has this to say;**

"She is, she is, she is one of the oldest Contessa of the area.  She
was, she was, she was here before the Open Curtain, and long before me. 
She shared the land with her sister, Contessa Palus.  They were Contessa
of the Grove and Contessa of the Fen.  Now they, now they, now they are
bitter enemies, and both are sad.  Both are, both are, both are
changed.  Both sad, or mad.  I can never tell between them."

**Contessa Fluvia of the Flowing Court**\
This is a dryad who came here after the Open Curtain (about 30 years
ago).  She is also called Contessa of the Brook.  Little is known of
her, and she hasn't been seen in a long time.

**Contessa Palus of the Sorrow Court**\
This is a hag that works with \"the Shadowkind\".  She has a great deal
of control over the Yellow Tooth, and is responsible for setting up the
little girls who were instrumental in The Tragedy.

*I note here this is no small discovery, though it seems to the Fey to
be of little consequence. Contessa Palus is the only living thing we can
be certain of that was involved in the Tragedy, and helped turned the
wheels that have ultimately brought us where we presently stand.*

Of **the Dol\'Rullian Troupe**, we were told the following;

Dol\'Rullian is a Grimstalker who has been here before.  He is attracted
to farms, and in particular, he wants to destroy Greenwood again.  He
took over the Limestone Court, and imprisoned Calix when she refused to
help him re-destroy Greenwood.

He has two fey sisters as his lieutenants, Vainya and Vallia.  Vallia is
reportedly the \'mean sister\'.

He has 4 Twigjacks and 16 Grimples (had 20) in his troupe.

This is a great deal for us to digest. We have just been getting
comfortable with the idea of fighting back the Yellow Tooth, and
wrestling with The Shadow. Now we have been drawn into some Fey
political struggle that will be impossible to understand fully, as these
beings do not think like us.

However, one thing is clear; the Sprites seem peaceful and could
potentially be allies. Dol'Rullian has vowed to destroy our farm and
kill our people. Tempus has show us a clear path.

## The Fey Among Us

Around midnight, Chrysalis returned to Camp with all of the news and
Sprites I have described above. There was some tension when the Captain
arrived, a Sprite sat atop her shoulder, and Ms. Autumhill's birds
hopping along behind the lot of them. The halfling was happy to be
reunited with her flock, and though her initial response to the Pixies
was anger, as I write this, they are all laughing together in the next
tent. Pixies by their nature enjoy halflings and elves, and can warm to
humans. Indeed, the Pixie known as Masra has barely left Jasper's
shoulder since they arrived.

The Men have been given strict instructions to take care of the Pixies;
to keep them safe but not handle or harass them. My sergeants have been
instructed that their chevrons depend on the safety and respect
delivered to the Fey, as these will be important allies in the months to
come. They could be a terrible foe otherwise, as we've already seen.

## Abbot Ambershields and the Cormyrean Pilgrims

Shortly after Chrysalis took off into the Wood to find Mason and
discover the Blue Glade, a train of pilgrims arrived in Camp. Abbot
Ambershields of the Cormyrean sect of Amaunator arrived, intent on
settling the region. Though I was surprised to see them, the Captain was
not. She had met them the previous tenday in Daggerfalls, and recounted
this discussion for me;

> *\"I\'m Abbot Aldrus Ambershields.  I have a small flock of Amaunatori
> that are looking for a fresh start.  We aren\'t exactly like your
> local sect, but we\'re good people, industrious and devout.  We could
> build you a church and a hospital if you could give us the land.  We
> could pay a modest tax on the property, if that\'s what\'s done.\"*
>
> *He is traveling with 2 other young abbots, and 3 Cormyrean families
> (a total of 17 people plus himself).*
>
> *You have a very good feeling about him.  You realize that he will
> fill the need to build a temple to Amaunator, without introducing the
> complicated hierarchy of the local church.  He seems very pastoral,
> rather than militant or rigid.  And they travel with a few wagonloads
> of supplies, including quite a bit of stuff for building and stocking
> a small hospital.*
>
> *\"Can I be of service to you?\" the kind middle aged man asks\...*

What exactly was promised and what will be granted to these pilgrims is
something we shall resolve on the morrow.

And as like attracts like, the Cormyrean Pilgrims have arrived with two
more local families in their train, also looking for a new life on the
edge of the wild.

The bravery of these pilgrims, countrymen and foreigners alike, is the
very light we hoped to kindle in this dark wood. Common men and women
who followed our example to set their own; braving a life on this
frontier rather than a campaign.

Today, Tempus has shown me that bravery comes in many forms.
