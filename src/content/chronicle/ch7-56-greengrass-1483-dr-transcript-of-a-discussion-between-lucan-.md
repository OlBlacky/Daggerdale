Lucan sits on an upturned stump of a log, staring at the small campfire
near the shore while the din of constant labour in Greenwood fades into
the background.

He is uncharacteristically silent: brooding and staring at the fire.

Jasper stands at the water's edge, a few metres away, looking up at the
countless stars above on this clear spring night.

"I agree with everything you've said," says the wizard.

"We've probably got the shape of the thing now. If these people weren't
your family, what happened to your real family? Is that whole business
about heading to Chessenta true, then?"

Jasper pauses to contemplate a Lucan doppelgänger-cousin strutting about
some pyramids complaining about the heat.

"In any case, what I'd like to know is when did Palus cross with the
Netherese? If the chapel dates from the early 1400s, her plan must've
been in place for quite some time. Was she the one that lured them here?
Was she in league with the shades already? Or maybe her plan came about
later and she hijacked the image and worship of Janath for her own
plans?"

Jasper looks down from the stars and toward the eastern horizon where
Thistle Ridge waits.

"I think it's time we get some real answers. The others are ready and
waiting for us. Let's mount up."

"Truffle pig?!" Jasper looks at Lucan who simply continues to stare at
the fire.

"I'll have you know a good truffle hunting pig in the Dales is worth
more than Ser Rowan's shiny new armour.  Be that as it may, I can't
disagree with any of what you've said..."

Jasper stares into the fire as well and is silent for a few minutes.

"What you're most right about is that we are being used. If we defeat
Palus, we will open the door for Mourvanesh's conquest if we aren't
strong enough to stop her at that point. It may not be what the Captain
wants to hear, but we might need to make an ally of Palus and not an
enemy."

A few more silent minutes of staring at the fire.

"Ok, but then answer me this... why?  Why any of it? Maybe I am just a
simple pig, but the deeper we dig looking for that truffle, the less I
seem to understand the "why" of it. Still makes me feel like we're the
ones that are going to be served up for supper at the Greengrass feast."

\"Aw, Jas. I didn\'t mean anything by it. I just mean you\'re diligent!
But you know, you\'ve got a point, kind of. Why did Palus turn on her
sister? That\'s a question for the wives, I suppose. Though I doubt fey
know each other\'s minds any better than we do.\"

**(Lucan laces his fingers together and stretches his arms toward the
fire, palms outward.)**

\"Melagaunt and Netheril now\...\" 

**(Lucan swirls his wine, his gaze fixed on the dancing flames. There\'s
a cynical edge to his voice, the sound of someone discussing a predator
they know all too well.)**

\"Right, Melagaunt Tanthul. One of the originals. And his interest in
Daggerdale\....\"

**(He takes a slow drink, his expression unreadable for a moment.)**

\"Look, I saw how they work, growing up in Saerloon. We weren\'t under
their thumb, but\... They trade above board and below, and you don\'t
mess with them either way. Netheril isn\'t impulsive. They\'re patient.
They plan. They reward service. And they come down hard on anyone who
thinks they can take advantage. They\'ve been a fact of life for my
family - my real family - for over a century now. And from what I\'m
told about Daggerdale, it\'s *always* been a strategic piece on the
board -- that pass, the proximity to Anauroch, the buffer against the
Moonsea. And now Tethyamar as a second route east\...\"

**(He gestures to the west, wincing as he spills his wine.)**

\"Why? Why *now*? Maybe internal politics shifted within that Shade
Enclave of theirs. Maybe the Zhents got too bold nearby, and the Princes
decided to swat them down by taking something that the Keep has always
wanted but could never have. Or maybe,\" **(shrugs)**, \"Daggerdale is
simply the next on their list. They took Sembia, they\'ve warred
elsewhere\... they move methodically and inexorably.\"

**(Lucan leans forward slightly, his eyes sharpen in a way that Jasper
recognizes from when he\'s on a case.)**

\"From their perspective, an independent Daggerdale - it may be small,
but it\'s a vulnerability. A potential staging ground for enemies like
the White Cloaks, a disruption to their trade routes to the Moonsea.
More importantly, Daggerdale is a symbol that defiance is possible. The
Zhents know that well. It\'s the Lordless Country. But the Netherese\...
they won\'t tolerate such things indefinitely. Crushing Dagger Falls
would bring the entire area more firmly under their administrative
control -- and they do like control.\"

\"And if Melagaunt is spearheading it? That just tells you it\'s a
high-level priority. He wouldn\'t be assigned to trivial matters. Maybe
it\'s a strategic goal for High Prince Telamont, or maybe Daggerdale is
just the final piece Melagaunt needs to declare *his* region fully
\'pacified\' and integrated into the Empire.\"

**(He leans back, sighing softly.)**

\"It\'s not about \'why Daggerdale\' in isolation. It\'s about
why *Netheril* does *anything*. Control. Resources. Strategic advantage.
Eliminating potential threats. It\'s the same pattern, played out over
decades. It could just be that Daggerdale\'s number finally came up.
That\'s the reality of living under the Shadow.\"

Lucan eyes take on an even more cunning gleam than usual.

\"Funny you should say that, Jasper. I\'ve been thinking on these girls
too, but, as Nemia informed us, Alaventa is a bit of an outlier.\"

Lucan passes Jasper his journal. \"Here\'s a page that may interest
you.\"
