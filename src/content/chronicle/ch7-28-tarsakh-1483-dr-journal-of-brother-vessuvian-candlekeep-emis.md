*Copy submitted to my friend, Jasper Hawkford, as well as to the Yellow
Abbeye Chronicle.*

I am not likely to go down in the history books as a hero, or mover or
shaker of anything in this age, except where I mention myself. However,
I have bore-witness to many of the crucial events of the Contest for
Thistle Ridge, the terrible conclusion to it, and the discussions that
followed shortly thereafter. I am not half the chronicler that is
Grafton Fitzdecker. Though, as a scholar I expect we are more or less
equals. And I am not half the scholar that Sr. Sarah Estelmer is, and in
fact have no qualities to boast of next to hers. But I was involved in
these events in my own small way, and had the fortune to accompany my
friend, Jasper Hawkford as his company, Chrysalis, navigated the
complexities of wrapping up the contest and starting the real business
of retaking Thistle Ridge. It was an honour to have been a fly on the
wall for these discussions, and I swear to both Oghma and Deneir that my
account is as true as a disciplined Brother of Candlekeep is capable.
